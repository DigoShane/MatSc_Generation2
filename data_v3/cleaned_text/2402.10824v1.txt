The Nonequilibrium Green's Functions (NEGF) theory provides a versatile method for addressing driven non-dissipative systems, utilizing the powerful diagrammatic technique to incorporate correlation effects. We here present a second-quantization approach to the dissipative NEGF theory, reformulating Keldysh ideas to accommodate Lindbladian dynamics and extending the Kadanoff-Baym equations accordingly. Generalizing diagrammatic perturbation theory for many-body Lindblad operators, the formalism enables correlated and dissipative real-time simulations for the exploration of transient and steadystate changes in the electronic, transport, and optical properties of materials.
