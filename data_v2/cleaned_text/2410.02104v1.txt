First-Principles Calculation of Superconducting Tc in Superhard B-C-N Metals Adam D. Smith,∗ Yogesh K. Vohra, and Cheng-Chien Chen† Department of Physics, University of Alabama at Birmingham, Birmingham, Alabama 35294, USA (Dated: October 4, 2024) We perform first-principles electron-phonon calculations to evaluate the superconducting transi- tion temperature Tc for ternary superhard metals B2C3N and B4C5N3. These materials are pre- dicted to exhibit a wide distribution of electron-phonon coupling parameters on their multiple Fermi surface sheets, which necessitates solving the anisotropic Eliashberg equations for accurate determi- nation of Tc. An ambient-pressure Tc of ∼ 40 K and ∼ 20 K is obtained respectively for B2C3N and B4C5N3 from the anisotropic gap equations. The relatively high Tc of these compounds is due in part to their high Debye temperatures associated with superhardness. The materials under study are potentially synthesizable, as their formation energies are comparable to those of other recently synthesized superhard B-C-N compounds. Therefore, studying superhard metals could hold the promise of realizing new higher-Tc superconductors at ambient pressure. Introduction – A recent resurgence of interests in studying phonon-mediated Bardeen-Coopper-Schrieffer (BCS) superconductors has largely resulted from the successful prediction and realization of superconduct- ing high-pressure hydrides H3S and LaH10 [1–5]. These achievements have greatly enhanced the prospects of room-temperature superconductivity. However, the re- quirement for high external pressures in the megabar (Mbar or 100 GPa) range to synthesize and maintain su- perconducting states in these materials limits their prac- tical applications. In addition to hydrogen-rich materi- als [6–8], other high-pressure systems based on light el- ements beryllium (Be), boron (B), carbon (C), and/or nitrogen (N) also have been studied [9–15]. Searching for new, synthesizable superconductors with higher Tc at re- duced pressure would promise to revolutionize the energy, transportation, and information technologies [16–18]. Generally, the Tc of phonon-mediated (BCS) super- conductors increases in materials with a large electron- phonon coupling strength λ, and/or a high Debye tem- perature ΘD . Recent theoretical studies have suggested that Tc ≤ AΘD for BCS superconductors, with the pre-factor A ∼ 0.1 . ΘD is typically elevated in highly compressed materials due to pressure-induced hardening. In the absence of external pressure, high ΘD can occur in materials with superior mechanical prop- erties, such as those exhibiting superhardness. In Vick- ers indentation test , hardness HV is determined by the ratio of the applied force to the resulting indentation area. A material is called superhard when its HV ≥ 40 GPa . At ambient pressure, diamond is the known hardest material with HV ∼ 100 GPa and a high ΘD ∼ 2200 K . However, diamond fails to become a BCS superconductor due to its large insulating gap. On the other hand, a metallic superhard compound, BC5, was theoretically suggested to show a relatively high Tc in the range ∼ 11 − 45 K, depending on the underlying crystal structures [27–29]. BC5 with a local covalently- bonded structure similar to cubic diamond can exhibit a high hardness HV ≥ 60 GPa . The Tc for other binary superhard compounds like BC3 and BC7 were also computed to be between ∼ 11−23 K . It remains an important experimental task to verify if these mate- rials can be synthesized in the proposed structures, and if they can superconduct at the high theoretical Tc. In this work, we perform first-principles electron- phonon calculations to study the superconducting Tc of B2C3N and B4C5N3, which were recently predicted to be superhard metals (with HV ≥ 40 GPa) by machine learn- ing and evolutionary structure searches . These mate- rials exhibit multiple nested Fermi surface sheets, which suggests anisotropic electron-phonon couplings and ne- cessitates solutions of the anisotropic Eliashberg equa- tions for a precise determination of Tc. The anisotropic calculations indicate Tc values of ∼ 40 K and ∼ 20 K for B2C3N and B4C5N3, respectively. Notably, the theoreti- cal formation energy of B2C3N and B4C5N3 is compara- ble to that of previously synthesized ternary superhard materials such as BC2N and BC10N . Therefore, it is feasible that the B-C-N materials under study could be synthesized by high-pressure high-temperature (HPHT) techniques or microwave plasma chemical vapor deposi- tion (MPCVD). Consequently, superhard metals could offer an intriguing new avenue for achieving higher-Tc BCS superconductors under ambient pressure. Methods – First-principles density functional the- ory (DFT) calculations are based on the Quantum ESPRESSO software (version 7.2) , and electron- phonon calculations are performed with the EPW code (version 5.7) [39–41]. In addition to B2C3N and B4C5N3, we perform benchmark calculations for BC5 and MgB2 (the current Tc record-holder among ambient-pressure BCS superconductors). under study. The BC5, B2C3N, and B4C5N3 structures are sourced from their respective papers and ar- ranged into 12-atom unit cells. The structure for MgB2 is sourced from the Materials Project . The Monkhorst- Pack sampling scheme is used with a Γ-centered k-point mesh of resolution 0.02 × 2π ̊A−1 along each reciprocal lattice vector. The convergence criteria of self-consistent and structural relaxation calculations are set to 10−8 Ry per unit cell and 10−6 Ry per Bohr radius, respectively. We adopt a wavefunction kinetic energy cutoff of 100 Ry, which suffices to converge the DFT total energy difference within 10−6 Ry per atom. For each crystal structure, we fully relax the lattice pa- rameters and atomic positions without pressure. After- wards, we compute the electronic, phonon, and electron- phonon properties. All calculations use norm-conserving pseudopotentials from the Pseudo Dojo library and the Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (PBE) exchange-correlation functional . The dynamical matrices and linear vari- ation of the self-consistent potential are calculated with density-functional perturbation theory (DFPT) . The EPW calculations use Wannier interpolation on uniform Γ-centered k-grids with the Wannier90 code . In the cases of BC5, B2C3N, and B4C5N3, we con- sider 2p orbitals for every B and C atom as initial projec- tions for the maximally localized Wannier functions ; in MgB2, Mg 3s and B 2pz orbitals are considered. Af- ter Wannierization, we perform electron-phonon compu- tation on dense 70×70×14 (70×70×70) k and 35×35 ×7 (35×35×35) Fourier-interpolated q grids for the B-C and B-C-N systems (MgB2) to evaluate the Eliashberg spec- tral function α2F(ω) and the isotropic electron-phonon coupling λ. The anisotropic Eliashberg equations are solved on the same dense interpolated k and q grids with imaginary Matsubara frequencies . The solutions are brought to the real axis with Pad ́e approximants. The crystal structures and Fermi surfaces are visualized re- spectively by VESTA and FermiSurfer softwares. Results and Discussion – Figure 2 shows the elec- tronic band structures and density of states (DOS). BC5, B2C3N, and B4C5N3 all exhibit local covalently bonded atomic arrangements similar to diamond. Therefore, they have similar band structures [Figs. 2(a)-(c)], but are effectively hole doped with the Fermi level (EF ) re- siding within the valence bands. Specifically, BC5 and B2C3N feature five bands crossing the EF with compa- rable electron DOS at the Fermi level (NF ). In contrast, B4C5N3 shows only three bands crossing the EF with a much lower NF . This variation in NF between B2C3N and B4C5N3 is expected, due to the differing hole dop- ing levels based on the valence electron counts of B and N atoms. A larger difference in the B-to-N ratio in the chemical composition will result in stronger hole doping. The electronic structure for MgB2 is shown in Fig. 2(d). There is a notable difference in the band topology between MgB2 and the other compounds under study. MgB2 is known to exhibit a complex mixture of σ and π orbitals, which manifest as both 2D and 3D Fermi surface sheets . Conversely, p orbitals dominate the bands near the EF in BC5, B2C3N, and B4C5N3. The varia- tion in NF suggests differences in how electrons interact with phonons in these materials, which play a vital role in determining their phonon-mediated superconductivity. high-symmetry paths of the materials under study. The anisotropic Migdal-Eliashberg (aME) theory using Wannier functions takes into account momentum-dependent interactions on the Fermi surface and variations in electron-phonon coupling across differ- ent phonon modes. By solving the aME equations on fine momentum and frequency grids, we can determine the anisotropic superconducting gaps on the Fermi sur- face to more accurately evaluate Tc. Figure 4 left pan- els show the resulting anisotropic gap functions and the Fermi surfaces of the materials under study. BC5 and B2C3N exhibit a similar Fermi surface topology and a single superconducting gap [Figs. 4(a)-(b)]. In contrast, B4C5N3 manifests two gaps similar to MgB2 [Figs. 4(c)- (d)]. B4C5N3 has a Fermi surface structure similar to BC5 and B2C3N, but with a flatter and more pronounced hexagonal central band and cylindrical outer band. In MgB2, the two gaps originate from the distinct 2D and 3D Fermi surfaces. Its σ bands are quasi-two-dimensional and show stronger electron-phonon coupling with low- energy phonon modes, causing a higher superconducting gap. The π bands are more three-dimensional, showing a weaker coupling and a lower superconducting gap. Finally, the anisotropic nature of the materials un- der study also can be seen from the distribution ρ(λ) of the momentum-resolved electron-phonon coupling on the Fermi surface λk, as shown in the right panels of Fig. 4. The width of ρ(λ) indicates the degree of anisotropy. In BC5 and B2C3N [Figs. 4(a)-(b)], while λk is peaked around 0.7 and 0.8, its distribution spans from 0.5 to 1.0. In B4C5N3 and MgB2 [Figs. 4(c)-(d)], the anisotropy is even more substantial due to the presence of two dis- tinct superconducting gaps. In each gap, λk is fairly anisotropic with a wide distribution. Such an anisotropy in λk leads to a highly underestimated Tc in the isotropic approximation of the electron-phonon coupling. Conclusion – We have performed detailed first- principles electron-phonon calculations to study super- hard metals BC5, B2C3N, and B4C5N3. Further theoretical explorations of Tc in different superhard metals via the anisotropic Eliashberg gap equations, alongside experimental inves- tigations of their synthesizability and superconducting properties, are important future research areas for discov- ering new higher-Tc phonon-mediated superconductors.
