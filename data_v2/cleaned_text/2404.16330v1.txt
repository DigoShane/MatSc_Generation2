Identifying the ground state phases by spin-patterns in the Shastry-Sutherland model Yun-Tong Yang,1, 2 Fu-Zhou Chen,1, 2 and Hong-Gang Luo1, 2, вИЧ 1School of Physical Science and Technology & Lanzhou Center for Theoretical Physics, Lanzhou University, Lanzhou 730000, China 2Key Laboratory of Quantum Theory and Applications of MoE & Key Laboratory of Theoretical Physics of Gansu Province, Lanzhou University, Lanzhou 730000, China Exploring the influence of frustration on the phases and related phase transitions in condensed matter physics is of fundamental importance in uncovering the role played by frustration. In the two-dimensional square lattice, a minimal frustration has been formulated in 1981 as the Shastry- Sutherland (SS) model described by competitions between the nearest-neighbor bond (J1) and the next-nearest-neighbor one (J2). In the two limits of ќ± = J2/J1, i.e. ќ± вЙ™ 1 and ќ± вЙЂ 1, the corresponding phases are the N ћБeel antiferromagnet (AFM) and the dimer-singlet(DS). Unfortunately, the intermediate regime remains controversial, and the nature of transition from the N ћБeel AFM to the intermediate state is also unclear. Here we provide a pattern language to explore the SS model and take the lattice size L = 4 √Ч 4 with periodic boundary condition. We firstly diagonalize the Hamiltonian in an operator space to obtain all fundamental spin-patterns and then analyze their energy and occupancy evolutions with the frustration parameter ќЇ = ќ±/(1+ќ±). Our results indicate that the intermediate regime is characterized by diagonal two-domain spin-pattern while the N ћБeel AFM state has a diagonal single-domain and the DS has mixings of diagonal single- and four- domain. While the transition from the DS to the intermediate phase occurred around ќ±c = 1.5 is the first-order in nature, consistent with that in literature, the one from the intermediate phase to the AFM is clearly seen around ќ±c = 1.277, where it has a reversal of the contributions from the single- and two-domain patterns to the ground state. The result indicates that the pattern language is powerful in identifying the possible phases in frustrated models. Introduction.вАФFrustrated magnetism has become an extremely active field in condensed matter physics due to finding potentially new states or new properties of this kind of matter . As a minimal frustrated model in the two-dimensional (2D) square lattice, the Shastry- Sutherland (SS) model has been extensively studied. This model was originally proposed in 1981 as an exactly solvable antiferromagnetic model with nearest- neighbor (J1) and next-nearest-neighbor (J2) couplings. A dimer-singlet (DS) state forms an exact eigenstate at all couplings and is the ground state in a region where J2 dominates . Interests in this model mainly come from two aspects. The first is the discovery of the quasi- 2D material SrCu2(BO3)2 in 1999 because the spin network of SrCu2(BO3)2 is topologically equivalent to the SS model . The second is that SrCu2(BO3)2 is a typical spin-gap system which may have relevance to the pseudogap behavior observed in the high-Tc cuprates superconductivity . The SS model is in the N ћБeel antiferromagnetic (AFM) state and the DS state at both ends of phase diagram. However, the intermediate regime with maximal frustra- tion remains controversial. Different candidate ground states were proposed by different approaches. For exam- ples, a direct transition from the N ћБeel AFM phase to the DS phase was obtained by means of series expansion and variational projected entangled pair states algorithm ; a helical state was addressed by using Schwinger boson mean-field theory and gauge-theoretic analy- sis ; a plaquette valence bond state (VBS) was first reported by means of series expansion in 2000 , then other methods such as exact diagonalization (ED) , density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) and tensor network method [17вАУ19] also give the same result. Futhermore, the plaquette VBS is further divided into full plaquette VBS [20вАУ22] and empty pla- quette VBS ; a columnar-dimer state, an in- commensurate spin-density wave phase and a topologi- cally ordered state were also proposed by means of series expansion , field theory and ED , respectively. Very recently, the coexistence of a plaquette VBS and a quantum spin liquid state between Ne ћБel AFM and DS was proposed by using DMRG , ED and func- tional renormalization group . Moreover, the nature of phase transition, particularly between the N ћБeel AFM phase and the intermediate phase, is also unclear. Is it a second-order transition or weakly first-order tran- sition , or other forms such as a deconfined quantum critical point ? Here we use the pattern language to provide some insights into the above controversies. The pattern lan- guage was proposed in our previous works [29вАУ35]. We have applied this language to one-dimensional and 2D frustrated spin models, namely, the axial next-nearest- neighbor Ising model (ANNNI) and the spin-1/2 J1- J2 Heisenberg model on a square lattice . The results provide an explicit physical picture for the maximal frus- tration regime of these two models. Naturally, we still employ the pattern language to study the SS model. For simplicity, we take lattice size L = Lx√ЧLy (Lx = Ly = 4) under periodic boundary condition (PBC). Summary and Discussion.вАФWe use the pattern lan- guage to explore the SS model with a small lattice size of L = 4 √Ч 4. By diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in an oper- ator space, we obtain six kinds of spin-patterns. Then we 5 employ spin-patterns to identify the ground state phases. Our results show that the ground state could be qualita- tively divided into three regions, where region I at small ќЇ and region III at large ќЇ are identified as the N ћБeel AFM phase and the DS one, respectively. The intermediate regime with maximal frustration has a feature of diago- nal two-domain structure. At ќ±c1 вЙИ 1.277, the SS model evolves from the the N ћБeel AFM phase to diagonal two- domain dominated phase. At ќ±c2 вЙИ 1.5, the model occurs a first-order phase transition from diagonal two-domain dominated phase to the DS one. Increasing lattice size, the pattern will show more abundant features, such as more domain structures. However, the phenomena that the phase diagram evolves from the N ћБeel AFM state to the intermediate state, then to the DS state with ќЇ increasing does not change, only the phase transition points are slightly shifted. This will be testified in the future study.
