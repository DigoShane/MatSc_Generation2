A microscopic twoвҖ“band model for the electron-hole asymmetry in high-Tc superconductors and reentering behavior J.-B. Bru, W. de Siqueira Pedra and A.-S. D МҲomel (Dated: March 11, 2022) To our knowledge there is no rigorously analyzed microscopic model explaining the electronвҖ“hole asymmetry of the critical temperature seen in highвҖ“Tc cuprate superconductors вҖ“ at least no model not breaking artificially this symmetry. We present here a microscopic twoвҖ“band model based on the structure of energetic levels of holes in CuO2 conducting layers of cuprates. In particular, our Hamiltonian does not contain ad hoc terms implying вҖ“ explicitly вҖ“ different masses for electrons and holes. We prove that two energetically nearвҖ“lying interacting bands can explain the electronвҖ“hole asymmetry. Indeed, we rigorously analyze the phase diagram of the model and show that the critical temperatures for fermion densities below halfвҖ“filling can manifest a very different behavior as com- pared to the case of densities above halfвҖ“filling. This fact results from the interвҖ“band interaction and intraвҖ“band Coulomb repulsion in interplay with thermal fluctuations between two energetic levels. So, if the energy difference between bands is too big (as compared to the energy scale defined by the critical temperatures of superconductivity) then the asymmetry disappears. Moreover, the critical temperature turns out to be a nonвҖ“monotonic function of the fermion density and the phase diagram of our model shows вҖңsuperconducting domesвҖқ as in highвҖ“Tc cuprate superconductors. This explains why the maximal critical temperature is attained at donor densities away from the maximal one. Outside the superconducting phase and for fermion densities near halfвҖ“filling the thermodynamics governed by our Hamiltonian corresponds, as in real highвҖ“Tc materials, to a MottвҖ“insulating phase. The nature of the interвҖ“band interaction can be electrostatic (screened Coulomb interaction), mag- netic (for instance some HeisenbergвҖ“type oneвҖ“site spinвҖ“spin interaction), or a mixture of both. If the interвҖ“band interaction is predominately magnetic then вҖ“ additionally to the electronвҖ“hole asym- metry вҖ“ we observe a reentering behavior meaning that the superconducting phase can only occur in a finite interval of temperatures. This phenomenon is rather rare, but has also been observed in the soвҖ“called magnetic superconductors. The mathematical results here are direct consequences of [J.-B. Bru, W. de Siqueira Pedra, Rev. Math. Phys. 22, 233вҖ“303 (2010)] which is reviewed in the introduction. I. Since the discovery of mercury superconductivity in 1911 a significant amount of superconducting materi- als has been found. This includes usual metals, like lead, aluminum, zinc or platinum, magnetic materials, heavyвҖ“fermion systems, organic compounds and ceram- ics. HighвҖ“Tc cuprate superconductors are among the most interesting superconducting materials for appli- cations. In spite of that, a general microscopic the- ory explaining conveniently their thermodynamics is still not available. For instance, the experimentally wellвҖ“ known electronвҖ“hole asymmetry of highвҖ“Tc superconduc- tors which usually show higher critical temperatures for donors of holes than for donors of electrons, is not clearly understood from the microscopic point of view. As far as we know no microscopic theory can give up to now some incontestable explanation of this fact unless one artificially breaks the electronвҖ“hole symmetry of models (for instance by imposing explicitly two different mass for electrons and holes). In fact, the most plausible expla- nation of the electronвҖ“hole asymmetry is given in and is also based on twoвҖ“band (Hubbard or tвҲ’J) models in the strong coupling regime. These studies are debat- able and we discuss them in relation with our approach in Section II after introducing our twoвҖ“band model. The usual BCS model and the strong coupling BCSвҖ“ Hubbard model have structural properties preventing their critical temperatures of superconductivity from be- ing asymmetric w.r.t. the density of donors. The strong coupling BCSвҖ“Hubbard shows вҖ“ at least and in contrast to the usual BCS model вҖ“ the typical (though symmet- ric) вҖңsuperconducting domesвҖқ of highвҖ“Tc materials for the critical temperature. Therefore, the first objective is to investigate generalizations of the strong coupling BCSвҖ“Hubbard in order to find plausible вҖ“ microscopic вҖ“ explanations for the electronвҖ“hole asymmetry in highвҖ“Tc cuprate superconductors. The (oneвҖ“band) strong cou- pling BCSвҖ“Hubbard is a good starting point for further investigations on highвҖ“Tc phenomenology, also because it correctly describes the nonвҖ“superconducting phase of cuprates near halfвҖ“filling, which is MottвҖ“insulating and not metallic as in usual superconductors. Another class of superconductors which would be in- teresting to study are ferromagnetic superconductors [9, p. 263-267]. Indeed, these materials exhibit some pecu- liar features not found in ordinary superconductors, one of these being the reentering behavior: The system be- comes superconducting below a critical temperature Оёc1 and then magnetically ordered but not superconducting below Оёc2 with Оёc2 < Оёc1. Additionally, coexistence of a ferromagnetic phase and superconductivity seems also to appear around Оёc2, at least for some ferromagnetic super- conductors. Motivated by the thermodynamic behavior of ferromagnetic superconductors, the second goal of this paper is to show that a magnetic interвҖ“band interaction can be responsible for a reentering behavior. We introduce a twoвҖ“band model by using two copies of the (oneвҖ“band) model HN defined by (3) and by adding an interвҖ“band interaction term. The parameters of the first band, called here the вҖңsвҖқвҖ“band, are chosen such that a superconducting phase appears at low enough temper- atures if no interaction with the second band is present. By contrast, the parameters of the second band, called the вҖңf вҖқвҖ“band, are such that without interaction with the вҖңsвҖқвҖ“band one would have a (generally ferromagnetic) MottвҖ“insulating phase at low temperatures. We prove that the thermodynamic behavior of such a twoвҖ“band model inherits of course properties of the thermodynamic behavior of the oneвҖ“band model HN de- scribed in , but also shows additional features: вҖў There is an electronвҖ“hole asymmetry w.r.t. the critical temperature of superconductivity which re- sults from the interвҖ“band interaction and intraвҖ“ band Coulomb repulsion in interplay with thermal fluctuations between the вҖңsвҖқвҖ“ and вҖңf вҖқвҖ“bands. вҖў Choosing the relative strength of parameters to each other according to experimental data about the energy levels of holes in CuO2 layers of highвҖ“Tc 4 cuprate superconductors, we show that this asym- metry is in favor of a doping with donors of holes. This is just what is seen in cuprates. вҖў There is a (rather small) set of parameters with a reentering behavior coming from a magnetic interвҖ“ band interaction. вҖў There is a kind of вҖңmicroscopic MeiГҹner effectвҖқ: The increase of the magnetic interвҖ“band interac- tion destroys superconductivity without need of any magnetization induced by an external magnetic field. It follows that a reentering behavior can also appear without any ferromagnetism. Our twoвҖ“band model is certainly not the final micro- scopic theory of highвҖ“Tc cuprate superconductors even if one includes a small kinetic part as explained above. However, we are convinced that the model and analy- sis presented here highlight microscopic processes play- ing a crucial role in which concerns the phenomenology of highвҖ“Tc materials вҖ“ or at least a relevant part of it. The method presented in and used here gives ac- cess to domains of the phase diagram usually difficult to reach via other standard mathematical tools. For in- stance, the existence of the superconductorвҖ“Mott insu- lator phase transition for nearвҖ“one fermion densities per lattice site (see Fig. 2) can neither be obtained by per- turbation theory nor by spin reflection positivity ar- guments. Indeed, the spin reflection positivity described for instance in cannot give access to such a phe- nomenon as it requires a fermion density exactly equal to one. Also, the regime in which the superconducting domes are observed corresponds to the choice О» вҲј Оі and they are never seen if О» is too small as compared to Оі. Thus, perturbative arguments around the purely BCS case О» = 0 are inadequate. The results given here may be rigorously obtained by renormalization group tech- niques, but this method of analysis would be вҖ“ from the technical point of view вҖ“ probably much more demanding than the approach of . As a final remark the oneвҖ“band and twoвҖ“band models discussed here should be seen as a kind of вҖңsolvableвҖқ class of models capturing important phenomenological aspects and from which one can implement physically more re- alistic models вҖ“ for instance by using perturbation the- ory. Indeed, a first natural extension of the twoвҖ“band model вҖ“ beyond the introduction of a small kinetic term as explained above вҖ“ is the addition of a HeisenbergвҖ“type interaction for fermions within the nonвҖ“superconducting band in order to spontaneously create a ferromagnetic phase and to get a reasonable microscopic theory of fer- romagnetic superconductors. THE TWOвҖ“BAND MODEL In order to fix ideas about the microscopic struc- ture of typical highвҖ“Tc materials we consider the case of cuprates. Then the last occupied orbital of the copper cations Cu++ = (Ar)3d9 is almost full, i.e., it can be modelled by a halfвҖ“filled band of holes (exactly one hole per lattice site).
