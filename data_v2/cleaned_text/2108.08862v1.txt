This effect leads to the existence of inhomogeneous parity-breaking ground states combining the chiral magnetic helix and the pair density wave orders. The formation of such states takes place via the penetration of chiral domain walls at the threshold temperature below the superconducting transition. We find regimes with both the single and the re-entrant transitions into the inhomogeneous states with de- creasing temperature. The predicted hybrid chiral states can be found in the existing structures with realistic parameters and materials combinations. Recently the interest in superconductor/ferromagnetic insulator (S/FI) structures has been growing rapidly. Experimental advances in fabrication of S/FI systems has resulted in the observation of the spin-split spec- trum of Bogolubov quasiparticles in Al/EuS systems1–4. The combination of the effective Zeeman field gen- erated by the magnetic proximity effect and super- conductivity leads to the long-range spin transport in EuS/Al systems5–8, non-trivial spin pumping effects in NbN/GdN9 and Nb/yttrium iron garnet (YIG)10 structures. Moreover recently the InAs/Al/EuS mul- tilayers combining superconductivity, ferromagnetism and spin-orbit coupling (SOC) have been proposed as the platforms for the realization of the topological superconductivity11,12. In this Letter we demonstrate that the interplay of superconductivity, ferromagnetism and SOC generates a non-trivial ground state characterized by the chiral spin order accompanied by the spatial modulation of the su- perconducting order parameter. Such state which we call the chiral pair density wave (CPDW) state can occur in S/FI systems with interfacial SOC induced by heavy metal Pt layers as shown in Fig.1 as well as in a wider class of magnetic superconductors with spin-singlet order parameter13–16 . The CPDW states break the parity symmetry and have several other qualitatively different features com- pared to the previously studied cryptoferromagnetic states17 in the weakly magnetic superconductors18 and ultra-thin metallic ferromagnetic films on top of the superconductor19,20. The mechanism which provides the forma- tion of CPDW state is the Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction21–23 (DMI) generated by the superconduct- ing spin currents. The corresponding DMI interaction energy has the form FDM = |ψ|2dk · (m × ∇km) (1) where m = m(r) is the magnetic texture, d1,2,3 are the DMI vectors and ψ is the s-wave spin-singlet supercon- ducting order parameter. The interaction between the superconductivity and magnetism provided by Eq.(1) is determined by the magnetization gradients. Therefore it is conceptually distinct from the mechanism which in- volves superconducting phase gradients discussed in the context of the Edelstien effect24–27, helical superconduct- ing states28,29, spontaneous currents30,31 and the anoma- lous Josephson effect32. The DMI provides the formation of magnetic spirals and skyrmions33,34 which are considered as the building blocs of the future spintronics devices35. Therefore con- trolling DMI with external parameters such as the volt- age and strain attracts significant attention36–39. The superconductivity-induced DMI (1) provides a plethora of different effects resulting in the control over the mag- netic textures with the help of the superconducting cor- relations. One can see that the domain walls first occur at Tpdw with the zero concentration, i.e. the infi- nite inter-wall distance Lpdw(Tpdw) = ∞. At lower tem- peratures T < Tpdw the finite concentration of domain walls, determined by the distance between the neigh- bour domain walls Lpdw, appears. In the direct anal- ogy with Abrikosov vortices in type-II superconductor the concentration of domain walls is determined by the balance between the negative energy of the individual wall and the repulsion between neighbour walls. This can be seen in Fig.3c,d which show with the red, green and blue curves the evolution of the CPDW state compo- nents θ(x), ψ(x) with the temperature approaching Tpdw as marked by crosses on the black curve Lpdw(T) in the panel Fig.3a. The scales of θ(x) and ψ(x) relative to the period or to the distance between neighbouring defects Lpdw decreases with approaching Tpdw. This means that the near Tpdw the CPDW state consists of the almost isolated defects with the growing distance between them Lpdw(T → Tpdw) → ∞. To summarize, we have discovered the CPDW hybrid states combining the array of chiral domain walls and the modulation of the superconducting order parameter. The CPDW state is generated by the DMI mediated by Cooper pairs which can be considered as the contribution to the energy from superconducting spin currents. (i) The CPDW state breaks the parity symmetry having definite sign of magnetic chirality.
