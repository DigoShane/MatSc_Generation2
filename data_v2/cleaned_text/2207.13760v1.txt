X-ray ptychography utilizes overlapping illuminations to reconstruct the object’s phase and absorption signal with spatial resolutions much smaller than the focus size. Usually, the illumination overlap is chosen to be between 50 % and 60 % in order to ensure high quality reconstructions at reasonable scan times and/or doses. Here, we experimentally demonstrate that ptychographic iteration with object instead of flat initialization allows for a significant reduction of the overlap with only a modest loss in reconstruction quality. This approach could prove beneficial for dose sensitive experiments and for rapid feedback overview scans. INTRODUCTION X-ray ptychography constitutes a rapidly developing imaging technique holding the record for spatial resolutions in X-rays. In 2D imaging spatial resolutions below 10 nm have been demonstrated and in 3D tomography an isotropic resolution as small as 16 nm has been reported . These resolutions are achieved by illuminating the object with a focused X-ray beam, scanning the object through the focus with overlapping illuminations and then exploiting the resulting redundant information in iterative reconstruction schemes such as the extended ptychographic iteration engine (ePIE) . Generally, an overlap of about 60 % is recommended for high quality reconstructions of the object’s complex wave field . Recently, we have shown that instead of using a flat initialization for ptychographic iteration a low resolution object representation, directly accessible from the measured diffraction patterns, can be utilized to initialize the iteration . This has the advantages of avoiding phase artifacts associated with large phase gradients, increasing the speed of convergence especially for bulky samples and relaxing the requirements for purposefully structuring the illuminations (i.e., the probe). In addition, this approach is compatible with a wide range of employed ptychographic iteration algorithms. In the following, we will investigate the performance of object initialization on ptychographic scans with smaller than recommended overlaps. The center-of-mass signals are proportional to the beam deflection, which is mainly caused by the phase gradients ∂Φ/∂x and ∂Φ/∂y of the object. However, in case the wavefront of the probe is not flat (e.g., the object is located out of focus) the probe will also contribute to the deflection of the beam , which can be corrected for . The two phase gradients can be combined to retrieve a low resolution estimate of the phase signal Φ(r) by anti-symmetric, non-iterative phase reconstruction , which avoids boundary effects, and once again appropriate interpolation. However, object initialization is also compatible with other reconstruction algorithms such as ePIE. RESULTS To demonstrate the benefits of object initializing, we use the ptychographic dataset of a fluid catalytic cracking particle (FCC_particle_FZP_11_dataset_id1.mat) available online . The dataset was measured at the cSAXS beamline of the Swiss Light Source. The particle was scanned with an X-ray beam of 6.2 keV that was focused to a focal spot of 81 nm (FWHM) with a Fresnel zone plate. The particle was located 0.87 mm downstream of the focus, where the beam had expanded to a size of D = 1.65μm. The 2344 scan points were arranged in a Fermat spiral and covered a scan area of 50 μm×30 μm with an average step size of R = 0.8μm. The diffraction images were measured with an in-vacuum Eiger detector placed 5.268 m downstream of the sample. For the reconstruction, the images were cropped to 512×512 pixels around the beam center, resulting in reconstructed pixel size of 27 nm. More details about experiments are available in the original work . With these scan parameters, the average linear overlap was 1−R/D = 51% . To emulate quick overview scans, we randomly remove some of the scan points and choose two configurations: In the first, we keep half of the scan points, in the second we only keep a quarter. With half the scan points, the average step size grows to 1.13 μm decreasing the average overlap to 31 %. Using only a quarter of the original points, the average step size increases to 1.6 μm and the overlap shrinks to 3 %. In the original scan, the scan points are close enough that the probe covers each point of the scan area, as shown in Fig. 1(a). The average dose for this scan was around 1.3×104 photons per pixel, fluctuating inside the scan area between 6×103 and 2.4×104 photons. The average doses for using half or quarter the number of scan points scale correspondingly. However, the dose fluctuations increase disproportionally compared to the full scan. Other regions see less than 500 photons per pixel. For the quarter scan, many scan points have only a very weak overlap and are de facto isolated. But also the high-resolution features are better reconstructed than previously. All three curves cross the 1-bit-line at a higher spatial frequency. For a scan that is purely limited by photon noise, the resolution is expected to be inversely proportional to the fourth power of the photon count . Without any other effects, we therefore expect the resolution for the half scan to increase by 21/4 = 119% and for the quarter scan by 41/4 = 141% compared to the full scan. For the full scan, the FRC resolution is 34.4 nm. For the half scan, the measured resolution of 41.4 nm is close to the estimated 34.4nm · 1.19 = 40.9nm. Finally for the quarter scan, the measured resolution of 55.9 nm is larger than the estimated 34.4nm·1.41 = 48.6nm. Thus, reducing the overlap from 51 % to 31 % while using object initialization affected the spatial resolution solely via increased photon shot noise. Only a further reduction to 3 % overlap affected spatial resolution by additional under-sampling, while the overall appearance (Fig. 5c) of the phase signal was only slightly affected. CONCLUSION We have confirmed that reducing the overlap of pytchographic illuminations severely degrades the quality of retrieved phase signals if a flat initialization for the object’s complex wave field is used. We demonstrated that this degradation can be significantly alleviated if a low resolution estimate for the object wave field is employed to initialize the ptychographic reconstruction. In fact, the visual appearance of retrieved phase signals was almost not affected even when reducing the overlap from 51 % to 3 %. However, we observed a reduction in the achieved spatial resolution compatible with the expectation from photon shot noise for 31 % overlap and an additional impact of under-sampling for 3 % overlap. Nevertheless, we showed a significantly increased robustness of the ptychographic reconstruction with object initialization. Overall the spatial resolution only declined from ≈35 nm for 51 % overlap to ≈56 nm for 3 % overlap for object initialization. This allows more flexibility to trade between scan speed, sample coverage, and reconstruction quality and could prove beneficial for dose sensitive experiments or quick overview scans for rapid feedback.
