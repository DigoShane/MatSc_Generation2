INTRODUCTION The study of dynamic effects in superconductors be- gan soon after the appearance of microscopic BCS theory of superconductivity.1 Using the BCS theory, Mattis and Bardeen have calculated the admittance of a superconduc- tor Y (О©,q).2 Later, Abrikosov, GorвҖҷkov and Khalatnikov have obtained the admittance for pure superconductors by us- ing the GreenвҖҷs function technique.3 This technique was ap- plied by Abrikosov and GorвҖҷkov to calculate the linear re- sponse of superconductors with impurities.4 In more de- tail, the theory of admittance has been later developed by Nam.5 In these papers, it has been shown that at low tem- peratures absorption is absent if the frequency of electro- magnetic field О© is less than 2вҲҶ. This means that the real part of admittance Re[Y (О©)] вүЎ Y вҖІ(О©) equals zero in the limit T вҶ’ 0 and О© < 2вҲҶ/Д§. If frequency О© exceeds 2вҲҶ, Y вҖІ(О©) in- creases with increasing the difference (О©вҲ’2вҲҶ). On the other hand, the intensive study of dynamic col- lective modes in superconductors, both in low- and high-Tc ones, is carried out in the last decade. A special attention is paid to the amplitude mode (AM), which is called often in literature the Higgs mode.6 This mode has been stud- ied theoretically long ago7вҖ“29, but only recently it was ob- served in experiments.30,31 A superconductor (Nb1вҲ’xTixN) was driven out of the equilibrium by a short laser pulse (ter- aherz frequency range) and the temporal evolution of the deviation ОҙвҲҶ(t) from the equilibrium value вҲҶ was detected by a weak probe signal in picosecond time interval. CONCLUSIONS We analyzed the admittance Y (О©) of short weak links of two types. The first one is a one-dimensional superconduct- ing wire with a local suppression of the superconducting or- der parameter |ОЁ|. This system resembles a phase-slip cen- ter or a one-dimensional Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell structure. We calculated Y and the impedance Z = Y вҲ’1 on the basis of non-stationary Ginzburg-Landau equations.57 Ac current through this wire induces a condensate momen- tum QО© and an inhomogeneity of |ОЁ| leads to branch imbal- ance nimb and to the appearance of another gauge-invariant quantity, the potential Ој, proportional to nimb. As we mentioned in the Introduction, the branch- imbalance, i.e., the unequal population of the electron- and hole-like branches of the excitation spectrum, arises in nonuniform superconductors when a conversion of the su- percurrent jS into the quasiparticle current jN takes place (see Refs. 52вҖ“55). The typical examples of such a conver- sion are the passage of the charge current through the S/N boundary,54,70,71 or collective phase mode, i.e., Carlson- Goldman mode,72 in uniform superconductors.55,73вҖ“75 In the latter case, nonuniform perturbations of the the cur- rents jN and jS propagate with a finite wave vector k con- verting into each other so that the total current density j = jN + jS is not perturbed, j = 0. The electric field E, which arises in the wire (see Fig. 1), is caused by both quantities, QО© and вҲӮxОј so that neither of these quantities can be neglected (compare with a recent paper, , where only the quantity Q вҲқ A was taken into account). The real part of the impedance Z вҖІ has a maximum at some frequency О©m which decreases with increasing sup- pression of |ОЁ|. We also analyzed ac properties of short Josephson S-c-S weak links. The admittance Y (О©) is described by an ex- pression that has been obtained on the basis of micro- scopic equations for quasiclassical GreenвҖҷs function in the Keldysh technique.42 The obtained dependence Y (О©) is valid in a wide range of the frequencies О© and tempera- tures T provided the Thouless energy ETh = D/L2 exceeds вҲҶ, T , and Д§О©. At low temperatures T , the absorption is absent (Y вҖІ = 0) if the energy of photons Д§О© is less than the lowest energy gap in the center of the constriction МғвҲҶ = вҲҶcosПҶ0. With increas- ing the difference (Д§О©вҲ’ МғвҲҶ), the absorption monotonously increases if the phase difference 2ПҶ0 is less than the phase difference 2ПҶc corresponding to a maximum of the Joseph- son current IJ = Ic. In the interval 2ПҶc < 2ПҶ0 < ПҖ, the depen- dence of Y вҖІ(О©) has a maximum. The hump in the obtained dependence Y вҖІ(О©) is much broader than the peak in absorption in a current-carrying superconductor.32 The mechanisms causing these max- ima are different. In the first case, the maximum stems from excitation of quasiparticles with energy range defined by Eq. (64). These quasiparticles are bound in a potential well within the constriction. In the second case, in , the peak is related to a resonance excitation of the Higgs mode by an ac field. The anomalous enhancement of the real part of the ad- mittance Y вҖІ(О©) at low frequencies О© described by Eq. (67), is caused by interference of Cooper pairs and quasiparti- cles with energies in the interval determined by Eq. (64). These quasiparticles experience multiple Andreev reflec- tions. In the ballistic case, the quasiparticles occupy An- dreevвҖҷs levels.76вҖ“78 In the diffusive case, these levels are broadened by impurity scattering44,79 so that the peaks in the DOS N(З«,x) corresponding to AndreevвҖҷs levels disap- pear, and the function N(З«,x) is a smooth function with sin- gularities at З« = вҲҶcosПҶ0 and З« = вҲҶ. In the latter case, anoma- lous behaviour of low energy quasiparticles results in a sin- gularity of dc conductance at V вҶ’ 0.79 The enhancement of Y вҖІ(О©) at low frequencies results in an enhancement of the supercurrent noise because the real part of admittance and spectral function of noise are con- nected by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. The anoma- lous noise in Josephson weak links has been studied in de- tail in many papers.80вҖ“85 Note an important circumstance. In a current-biased S-c-S JJ, the states with phase difference 2ПҶc < 2ПҶ0 are un- stable, so that it is impossible to observe a non-monotonous dependence of absorption in these junctions. However, in recent experiments58,59 it was shown that the phase differ- ence 2ПҶ0 in the interval 2ПҶc < 2ПҶ0 < ПҖ (72) is reachable. Thus, it would be interesting to observe a non- monotonous dependence of absorption in such JJs by ap- propriate adjustment of the phase difference 2ПҶ0 with the help of an external magnetic field.
