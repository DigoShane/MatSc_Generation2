I. INTRODUCTION The perturbative finiteness in quantum field theory, particularly in Chern-Simons models in three space-time dimensions, has drawn attention since the preliminary results at 1-loop order , and afterwards at 2-loops . At all orders in perturbation theory, pure non-Abelian Chern-Simons model in the Landau gauge exhibits ultraviolet finiteness . However, even though coupled to bosonic and fermionic matter fields, non-Abelian Chern-Simons model in three-dimensional Riemannian manifolds still manifests at all radiative order vanishing ő≤-function associated to Chern-Simons coupling constant . The massless U(1) QED3 exhibits ultraviolet and infrared perturbative finiteness, parity and infrared anomaly free at all orders . Moreover, in opposition to some claims in the literature still now defending that parity could spontaneously be broken, even perturbatively, in massless U(1) QED3, known as parity anomaly, has already been discarded by the consistent and correct use of dimension regularization , Pauli-Villars regularization , algebraic renormalization in the framework of Bogoliubov-Parasiuk-Hepp-Zimmermann-Lowenstein (BPHZL) subtraction method , and more recently through the Epstein-Glaser method . The exact quantum scale invariance in dimensional reduced to three dimensional space-time massless QED4 models was investigated in , and the gauge covariance of the massless fermion propagator was studied in quenched QED3 . The massive U(1) QED3 can be odd (odd fermion families number) or even (even fermion families number) under parity symmetry. The parity-even massive U(1) QED3 is ultraviolet finite, the gauge coupling ő≤-function and the anomalous dimensions of all the fields vanish, furthermore, is infrared and parity anomaly free at all orders . Besides all the latter quantum field theory formal aspects, planar quantum electrodynamics (QED3) has been demonstrated potential applications in condensed matter phenomena and low energy physics, on the other hand in early universe models and high energy physics as well. The main purpose in this work is to show the ultraviolet finiteness ‚Äď vanishing ő≤-functions of both gauge couplings and all field anomalous dimensions ‚Äď at all orders in perturbation theory of the parity-even UA(1) √ó Ua(1) massive QED3 , and the absence of any kind of anomaly, e.g. gauge and parity, as well. The proof is done by using the BRS (Becchi-Rouet-Stora) algebraic renormalization method in the framework of Bogoliubov-Parasiuk-Hepp-Zimmermann (BPHZ) subtraction scheme, which is based on general theorems of perturbative quantum field theory [14‚Äď18], thus independent of any regularization scheme. Accordingly, the action of the model and its symmetries, the action for the gauge-fixing and the one which couples antifields to the nonlinear BRS transformations of the fields are established in Section II. The extension of parity-even UA(1)√óUa(1) massive QED3 at the classical level to all orders in perturbation theory ‚Äď its perturbative quantization ‚Äď is arranged as follows.
