Since the discovery of a superconducting high- entropy alloy (HEA) in 2014, the materials research on HEA superconductor is a hot topic. We have found that Hf21Nb25Ti15V15Zr24 body-centered-cubic (BCC) HEA is a new superconductor with the superconducting critical tem- perature Tc of 5.3 K. We briefly discussed the comparison of cocktail effect of Tc among BCC HEA superconductors. Introduction There are two well accepted definitions for high-entropy alloys: alloys containing at least five elements with the atomic percentage of i-th element Xi 5% ≤ Xi ≤ 35%, and alloys with the mixing entropy Smix larger than 1.5R, where R is the gas constant. Smix for a n elements alloy is given by −R �n i=1 xilnxi, where xi is the mole fraction of i-th component. A high-entropy alloy (HEA) generally forms a simple crystalline structure like face-centered-cubic, body-centered-cubic (BCC) or close-packed hexago- nal. Focusing on the superconducting HEAs, BCC materials are well studied, however, there are only several reports. This fact motivated us to carry out the materials research on HEA superconductors. Recently equimo- lar BCC HfNbTiVZr is reported, however, a secondary phase appears dur- ing annealing. We have searched a BCC Hf-Nb-Ti-V-Zr HEA, which is stable even after annealing, by changing the atomic composition, and checked the possible superconductivity. In this article, we report a thus found HEA su- perconductor Hf21Nb25Ti15V15Zr24. 2. Materials and methods The polycrystalline sample Hf21Nb25Ti15V15Zr24 was made by a home- made arc furnace. The constituent elements of Hf (99.9%), Nb (99.9%), Ti (99.5%), V (99.9%) and Zr (99%) with the stoichiometric composition were placed on a water-cooled Cu hearth and arc-melted in an Ar atmosphere. The annealing condition was 800 ◦C for 4 days in an evacuated quartz tube. T base c plays a role of base line in order to know an enhancement of Tc due to a mixing of constituent elements, and a larger T obs. c /T base c indicates a strong cocktail effect. VEC is an important factor deciding Tc, that is well known as the Matthias rule, in which the VEC dependence of Tc for crystalline d-electron systems shows a maximum at approximately VEC=4.5 and Tc steeply decreases to zero around VEC=4.0 or 5.3. However, the BCC HEA superconductors preserve a rather high Tc compared to the predic- tion value of the Matthias rule especially at the side of VEC smaller than 4.5. Keeping this in mind, we made a three-dimensional bar graph in Fig. 3 by using the numerical values listed in Table 1. The enhancement of Tc in each HEA is stronger than that of Nb66Ti33. The value of T obs. c /T base c seems to increase with increasing Smix. We note here that (TaNb)0.16(ZrHfTi)0.84 shows the strong Tc enhancement, although Smix/R of this compound is rel- atively small. VEC of (TaNb)0.16(ZrHfTi)0.84 is 4.16, where Tc is suppressed to nearly zero in the VEC dependence of Tc by the Matthias rule. This result suggests that the VEC also somewhat affects the enhancement of Tc in HEA superconductors. 4. Summary We have found that Hf21Nb25Ti15V15Zr24 is a new member of HEA su- perconductors. It crystallizes into the BCC structure with a=3.401 ̊A, and Tc is 5.3 K.
