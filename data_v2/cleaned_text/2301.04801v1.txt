Shibauchi1, † 1Department of Advanced Materials Science, University of Tokyo, Kashiwa, Chiba 277-8561, Japan 2Laboratoire des Solides Irradi ́es, CEA/DRF/IRAMIS, Ecole Polytechnique, CNRS, Institut Polytechnique de Paris, F-91128 Palaiseau, France 3Advanced Science Research Center, Japan Atomic Energy Agency, Tokai, Ibaraki 319-1195, Japan The paramagnetic spin-triplet superconductor UTe2 has attracted significant attention because of its exotic superconducting properties including an extremely high upper critical field and possible chiral superconducting states. Recently, ultraclean single crystals of UTe2 have become available, and thus measurements on these crystals are crucial to elucidate the intrinsic superconducting properties. Here, we report the thermodynamic critical field Hc, the lower critical field Hc1, and the upper critical field Hc2 at low fields of these high-quality single crystals. From the comparison of the anisotropies in Hc1 and Hc2, we find that the experimental Hc1 values with the magnetic field along b- and c-axes are anomalously enhanced, showing unusual low-temperature upturns. We propose an effect of the strong Ising-like ferromagnetic fluctuations on the vortex line energy as the origin of the anisotropic enhancement of Hc1. The superconducting state of UTe2 has been inten- sively investigated because of its spin-triplet nature with a paramagnetic normal state . The spin-triplet pair- ing state induces an extremely high upper critical field (Hc2) beyond the Pauli limit and multiple super- conducting phases under hydrostatic pressure or mag- netic field [4–9]. Furthermore, chiral spin-triplet states are discussed in scanning tunneling microscopy, polar Kerr effect , surface impedance , and penetration depth studies . As for the pairing interactions, the anisotropic Hc2 and reentrant superconductivity suggest that ferromagnetic fluctuations are at play anal- ogous to the ferromagnetic superconductors , which is supported by the nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) and muon spin rotation (μSR) measurements[16–18]. On the other hand, recent neutron scattering studies revealed the presence of antiferromagnetic fluctuations related to superconductivity [19–21]. Thus, the nature of magnetic fluctuations and their relationship to superconductivity are still unclear. What has complicated systematic understandings of the superconducting state in UTe2 are the inhomogene- ity of crystals and the presence of magnetic impurities. Single crystals grown by the conventional chemical va- por transport (CVT) method sometimes show double su- perconducting transitions likely induced by inhomogene- ity and a small increase in magnetic suscep- tibility at low temperatures, which reflects the presence of U vacancies acting as magnetic impurities [1, 18, 23– 25]. Recently, Sakai et al. developed another crystal growth method called the molten salt flux (MSF) method and succeeded in obtaining high-quality single crystals of UTe2 with a transition temperature Tc = 2.1 K, a resid- ual resistivity ratio as high as 1,000, and low magnetic impurity density . In these ultraclean crystals, novel superconducting and normal state properties have been reported in addition to the observation of de Haas-van Alphen oscillations . Therefore, measurements on the high-quality single crystals are crucial to clarify the superconducting nature in UTe2. In this study, we investigate the thermodynamic crit- ical field (Hc), the lower critical field (Hc1), and Hc2 in high-quality MSF single crystals. Regarding the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory, the anisotropies in Hc1 and Hc2 should be opposed because the critical fields sat- isfy Hc1Hc2 = H2 c (ln κ+0.5), where κ is the GL parame- ter, and Hc is independent of field directions. In contrast, we find that the anisotropy of Hc1 does not follow the ex- pectations from that of Hc2. Quantitatively, the above GL relation holds for H ∥ a, while it is obviously vio- lated for H ∥ b. Furthermore, Hc1 for H ∥ b and H ∥ c show unusual upturns at low temperatures. To explain these anomalous behaviors of the critical fields, we pro- pose an anisotropic enhancement of Hc1 induced by the Ising-like ferromagnetic fluctuations in UTe2. High-quality single crystals are grown by the MSF method as described in Ref. . Crystals #A1 and #A2 in this study are the same samples as crystal #A1 in Ref. and crystal #M7-1 in Ref. , respectively, and crystal #A3 for the Hc2 measurement is picked up from the same batch with the crystal used in the de Haas- van Alphen experiments . Crystals #A1 and #A2 have a cuboid shape with dimensions 455 × 250 × 95μm3 and 1400 × 285 × 165 μm3, respectively. Hc1 is measured by a miniature Hall-sensor array probe tailored in a GaAs/AlGaAs heterostructure[30–33]. The distance of neighboring sensers is 20 μm so that we can measure the local magnetic induction and the first flux penetration field Hp near crystal edges. In the previous study, because the critical fields violated the above GL relations, the authors assumed a magnetic field effect on Tc, dTc/dH . However, our results consistent with the GL relations for H ∥ a indicate that the dTc/dH effect is negligible as long as we focus on the initial slopes of the critical fields. In contrast to Ha c1, however, the initial slope of Hb c1 is significantly larger than the expected Hcal c1 value. In addition to the steep initial slope, Hb c1 and Hc c1 in ultra- clean UTe2 show an unusual increase below 0.5 K, which has not been observed previously. A similar Hc1(T ) be- havior was reported in multi-band iron-based supercon- ductors and the chiral superconductor candidates, UPt3 and PrOs4Sb12 . We emphasize that the increase of Hc1(T ) at low temperatures in these materials was observed regardless of the magnetic field directions, which is clearly different from the case in UTe2 where the increase is discernible only in H ∥ b and H ∥ c. Moreover, magnetic penetration depth measurements in PrOs4Sb12 also show a kink at lower temperatures , while the anisotropic penetration depth in UTe2 shows smooth T dependence around 0.5 K . Thus, while the enhancement of Hc1 at low temperatures in the previ- ous studies on the various superconductors reflects the increase of superfluid density, the anomalous Hc1(T ) in UTe2 likely has a different origin which induces a large anisotropy. Because the Hc1 value is determined by the vortex line energy, the unusual Hb c1(T ) and Hc c1(T ) behaviors can be related to an exotic vortex state. As for the vor- tices in UTe2, recent scanning SQUID measurements on the (011)-plane observed pinned vortices and antivortices even in a zero-field cooling condition, which can be re- lated to the presence of strong and slow magnetic fluc- tuations detected in μSR and NMR studies . More- over, optical Kerr effect measurements on the (001)-plane suggest the presence of magnetized vortices induced by strong magnetic fluctuations. From these results, we con- sider that the enhancement of Hb c1(T ) and Hc c1(T ) is re- lated to the strong Ising-like ferromagnetic fluctuations along a-axis inducing an exotic vortex state in UTe2. We note that anomalous low-T behavior has been also de- tected in 1/T1T of NMR measurements and zero-field relaxation rate of μSR measurements . The relationship between strong magnetic fluctuations and the enhancement in Hc1 has been investigated in BaFe2(As1−xPx)2 . In this system, an antiferromag- netic quantum critical point (QCP) appears at x = 0.3 where λ(0) shows a sharp peak reflecting the strong mass enhancement . In contrast, Hc1 also shows a peak at QCP, contradicting with the expected behavior from Eq. (2). This discrepancy suggests that the vor- tex line energy is significantly enhanced by the strong magnetic fluctuations near QCP. Thus, considering the presence of strong anisotropic ferromagnetic fluctuations in UTe2, the vortex line energy can be anisotropically enhanced by the Ising-like magnetic fluctuations. In- deed, the normal-state NMR measurements in UTe2 reported that 1/T1T shows a pronounced low-T enhance- ment for H ∥ b and H ∥ c, which is absent for H ∥ a. This shows a good correspondence with the anisotropic Hc1 enhancement. In conclusion, we measured the critical fields, Hc(T ), Hc1(T ), and Hc2(T ), in high-quality single crystals of UTe2 with Tc = 2.1 K. While the three critical fields for H ∥ a satisfy the usual GL relations near Tc, the experi- mental Hb c1 and Hc c1 are larger than the expected values, which become more apparent below 0.5 K. These results indicate that the anisotropic ferromagnetic fluctuations in UTe2 significantly enhance the vortex line energy with H ∥ b and H ∥ c. Our experimental results not only suggest that an exotic vortex state caused by anisotropic ferromagnetic fluctuations is realized in UTe2, but also promote further studies on magnetic fluctuations in high- quality single crystals of UTe2 with low magnetic impu- rity density. POSITION DEPENDENCE OF THE LOCAL MAGNETIC INDUCTION When the surface pinning or geometrical barriers of superconducting vortices are present, Hp is much en- hanced from Hc1 value and shows a large position de- pendence . To consider this effect, we measured the position dependence of Hp. Figure S6(a-c) shows lo- cal magnetic induction near edges (open squares) and in the bulk (filled circles) as a function of external magnetic field at 1 K for H ∥ a, H ∥ b, and H ∥ c, respectively. Because the distance of neighboring Hall bars is 20 μm, the bulk data reflect the magnetic induction away from the crystal edges by at least 20 μm. Obviously, we find that the Hp values are almost independent on the posi- tions. This result support that our measurements are not affected by the surface pinning or geometrical barriers of the superconducting vortices. Regarding the Bean model, the magnetic induction B satisfies the relation, B ∝ (H − Hp)2, above Hp. Thus, we fitted B1/2(H) with a linear function in a B > 0 region and defined Hp as the crossing point of the fitting line and B = 0.
