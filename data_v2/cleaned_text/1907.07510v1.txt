Buzdin2, 1, § 1Department of Materials Science & Metallurgy, University of Cambridge, CB3 0FS Cambridge, United Kingdom 2University Bordeaux, LOMA UMR-CNRS 5798, F-33405 Talence Cedex, France (Dated: July 18, 2019) Spin-orbit coupling (SOC) is known to play an important role in superconductor/ferromagnet het- erostructures. Here we demonstrate that SOC results in the spontaneous generation of vortices in an s-wave superconductor placed below a ferromagnetic metal with intrinsic or interfacial SOC. By us- ing the generalized London and Ginzburg-Landau theories, we calculate the supercurrent amplitude in various types of S/F heterostructures at and below the superconducting critical temperature. In particular, we show that even in the limit of weak SOC, vortex-antivortex pairs are stabilized and that an attractive interaction between vortices and SOC act to pin vortices along the S/F interface. We discuss key experiments to investigate these phenomena, which would provide a platform to the creation of Abrikosov vortex memory. In an s-wave superconductor (S), charge currents flows in the absence of dissipation but since the Cooper pairs con- sist of electrons with antiparallel spins in a spin-singlet state, the current does not carry net spin. The oppo- site occurs in a ferromagnetic (F) metal in which an internal exchange field hex creates a mismatch in the density of states of electrons with up and down spins, meaning that charge flow with a net spin-polarization but with with dissipitation . At a S/F interface, hex interacts within S over the superconducting coherence length which for Nb is ξS = 30 − 40 nm , whilst in F the interaction is much shorter with ξF = 1 − 3 nm in Co [3–5], Fe , Ni [3–5] due to hex acting to rapidly dephase the singlet pairs. Consequently, there exists a F-thickness-dependent modulation of the criti- cal temperature Tc in S/F bilayers and critical cur- rent Ic oscillations in S/F/S Josephson junctions [8–10]. total exchange field acting on the superconductivity is controllable via magnetization-alignment of the F layers, which creates a dependence of Tc on magnetic configu- ration or Ic in S/F1/F2/S Josephson junctions [20–22]. Furthermore, a non-parallel alignment of magnetizations between the F layers converts singlet pairs to a spin- aligned triplet state [23–25]. Since spin-aligned triplet pairs are spin-polarized, triplet supercurrents are able to carry a net spin in addition to charge and so are stable in F materials over length scales that exceed ξF [26–32]. These phenomena form the basis of applications in su- perconducting spintronics . Spin-orbit coupling (SOC) is also present at a S/F interface due to the absence of inversion symmetry [23, 24, 34–37] and can be enhanced by introducing an interlayer between the S and F layers with strong SOC. Since spin is not conserved in the presence of SOC, the SOC locally disrupts the singlet pairs, rotating their spins towards the direction of magnetization. Hence, SOC in combination with a single F layer can gener- ate spin-aligned triplet pairs . For example, in a recent ferromagnetic resonance spin-pumping experi- ment on Pt/Nb/Py [40–42] spin-pumping efficiency was enhanced over the normal state below Tc. These results indicated the presence of a spin-triplet channel in Nb that exists over ξS, which forms due to SOC in Pt in combination with hex from Py. Interfacial SOC provides a Rashba-type contribution ∼ (⃗σ × ⃗p) · ˆn to the energy, where ⃗σ is the electron spin, ⃗p is the orientation of the momentum and ˆn is the unit vector along the broken inversion symmetry axis. Mag- netic order spin-polarizes electrons, meaning that mo- mentum along ⃗σ × ˆn is energetically favourable and so may induce spontaneous charge currents. This is pre- dicted near a S/F interface within the London penetra- tion depth from the interface , around magnetic is- lands on a thin-film S , in a closed S loop partially covered by a ferromagnetic insulator and in a thin- film S in contact with a Ne ́el skyrmion . In this Letter we investigate the interaction of SOC with vortices in S/F heterostructures, at temperatures T below and at Tc. We derive a criterion for sponta- neous vortex generation due to SOC and show that this is achievable using realistic materials parameters. Based on these results, we demonstrate that SOC strongly af- fects transport properties in S/F junctions. In summary, we have theoretically investigated the role of SOC in S/F devices at and below Tc. We have shown that even weak SOC (α ≃ 0.01/ξ) generates vortex-antivortex pairs and that an attractive interaction between vortices and SOC acts to pin vortices along S/F interfaces. Vortex generation and pinning are prominent when the magnetization is perpendicular to the interface and diminishes as the magnetization rotates in-plane towards the S/F interface. In our calculations, we con- sider a F metal with intrinsic SOC; however, matching behaviour is expected when separating the SOC from the F layer by introducing a metallic interface with intrinsic SOC . Ideal materials combinations include Pt/Co, Pd/Co, Pt/Py and Pd/Py, which have high interfacial transparency with Nb . A key breakthrough of our theory is that it predicts the potential for vortex guiding without the usual requirement of stray magnetic fields to generate vortex-antivortex pairs and that vortex motion [see Fig. 1(a)] is reversible with applied current direction or magnetization which are key requirements for Abrikosov memory.
