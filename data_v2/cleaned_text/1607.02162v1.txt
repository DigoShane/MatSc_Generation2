The theory of the superconductivity for the e-band with the not constant density of electronic states The calculation of the superconducting transition temperature in the hydrogen sulphide 1,2 is usually carried out either by using extremely lax and insufficiently reasoned description of superconductivity with a freely selectable electron density functional вҲ’ without any possibility of taking into account the effect of nonlinear nature of Гүliashberg equations either within the approximate solutions of the Гүliashberg equations вҲ’ not taking into account the variable nature of the electronic density of states ( ) N Пү . The solution of the Гүliashberg equations on the real axis is considered to be a difficult task. Conclusions Analyzing the results and summing everything written before, we arrive at the following conclusions: 1. Гүliashberg theory is generalized to account for the variable nature of the electron density of states. 2. The mathematical method for the solving of Гүliashberg equations on the real axis is developed. 3. The generalized Гүliashberg equations with the variable nature of the electron density of states are solved. The quantitative agreement with the experiment for the hydrogen sulphide 3 SH phase is achieved. The fact of the very slow convergence of the solutions of Гүliashberg equations with increasing iteration number is established. 4. The frequency dependence as well as the fine structure of both the real part ( ) ReПҶ Пү and the imaginary part ( ) ImПҶ Пү of the order parameter corresponding to the selected 3 SH sulfide phase at the temperatures T = 175K , T = 180K, T = 300K is obtained. The variation of the frequency dependence of the order parameter with the temperature is presented. 5. The energy gap magnitude of the 3 SH sulfide phase is found to have the value 592 degrees. 6. It is shown that at the temperatures above the critical temperature the order parameter very slowly tends to zero with increasing the number of iterations, maintaining the characteristic functional behavior of the 16 superconducting state on the frequency. 7. The method of solving of the Гүliashberg equations on a set of discrete points on the imaginary axis, faced with the problem of the convergence of the solution at low order of the discrete matrix, does not accurately reproduce the dependence of the order parameter on the frequency after the analytic continuation of the order parameter to the real frequency axis compared to the method of solving of Гүliashberg equations on the real axis. It is shown that at the temperatures above the critical one the order parameter is very slowly tending to zero with increasing iteration number, maintaining the characteristic functional behavior of the superconducting state on the frequency. 8. All the calculations were carried out ab-initio. The work is not using any assumptions or any fitting parameters. The entire treatment was carried out on the real axis so as to be able to explore the frequency behavior of the order parameter simultaneously with the cT calculation without the analytic continuation procedure. We got 177 cT K = coinsiding with the experimental 2 cT value in the hydrogen sulfide at the pressure 225GPa. At the temperature 180 c T K T = > and even at the room temperature T = 300 K, when the equation for the order parameter leads to an extremely small maximum values of the order parameter 8 Re , Im 10 ПҶ ПҶ вҲ’ вҲј on the hundredth iteration, the order parameter frequency dependence is similar with the order parameter dependence on frequency for the superconducting state. 9. Three factors influence the cT of the EP system in the critical mode, namely: the variable nature of the electron density of states N0 ( ) Оө , the specific properties of a substance depending on the electron mass renormalization ( ) Re Z z , the damping ( ) Im Z z of the electrons, both the real ( ) Re ПҮ Пү and the imaginary ( ) Im ПҮ Пү components of the renormalization of the chemical potential, as well as the novel contributions ( ) Im вҲҶ Пү вҲј to the Гүliashberg equations which were not taken into account in the formalism of the previous works 12 вҲ’ 24 .The neglect of the terms proportional to 17 ( ) Im вҲҶ Пү leads to the violation of the Kramers-Kronig relations for the imaginary and the real part of the order parameter in the Гүliashberg equations. 10. It is of critical importance to take into account the variability of the density of electron states in the conduction band for the high cT appearance in the EP system. The accounting of the electron density of states variability in the band leads to the possibility of the pairing of electrons in the entire Fermi вҖ“ volume in contrast to the usually considered pairing within the layer with D Пү thickness at the Fermi surface.11. The Coulomb pseudopotential of electrons in the hydrogen sulfide leads to the inessential cT reduction. From the present consideration, given the considerable 592 degrees value of the superconducting gap and taking into account the conservation of the diminishing order parameter structure for the temperature larger cT , it becomes clear that the hydrogen sulfide EP system even at the temperatures above the critical point remains in the "quasi superconducting " state. From this it follows that the cT value in the EP system can be greatly improved as compared to the experimentally determined cT value in the hydrogen sulfide by changing the pressure and with the selection of optimal ( ) ( ) Re ,Im , Z Z Пү Пү ( ) Re ПҮ Пү , ( ) Im ПҮ Пү behavior, along with the optimal behavior of the electron density of states N0 ( ) Оө with a weak electron-hole non- equivalence and a moderate value of the EP interaction. It is also possible that the high temperature superconductivity can be achieved with the influence on the EP system being in a "quasi superconducting " state with the unknown specific weak perturbation the nature of which is still to be established.
