Directional Detection of Light Dark Matter with Polar Materials Sinead Griffin,1, 2 Simon Knapen,1, 3 Tongyan Lin,1, 3, 4 and Kathryn M. Zurek1, 3, 5 1Department of Physics, University of California, Berkeley, CA 94720, USA 2Molecular Foundry, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, Berkeley, CA 94720, USA 3Theoretical Physics Group, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, Berkeley, CA 94720, USA 4Department of Physics, University of California, San Diego, CA 92093, USA 5Theoretical Physics Department, CERN, Geneva, Switzerland Abstract We consider the direct detection of dark matter (DM) with polar materials, where single production of optical or acoustic phonons gives excellent reach to scattering of sub-MeV DM for both scalar and vector mediators. Using Density Functional Theory (DFT), we calculate the material-specific matrix elements, focusing on GaAs and sapphire, and show that DM scattering in an anisotropic crystal such as sapphire features a strong directional dependence. For example, for a DM candidate with mass 40 keV and relic abundance set by freeze-in, the daily modulation in the interaction rate can be established at 90% C.L. with a gram-year of exposure.
