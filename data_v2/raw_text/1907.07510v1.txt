Generating superconducting vortices via spin-orbit coupling

L. A. B. Olde Olthof,1, вҲ— X. Montiel,1, вҖ  J. W. A. Robinson,1, вҖЎ and A. I. Buzdin2, 1, В§

1Department of Materials Science & Metallurgy, University of Cambridge, CB3 0FS Cambridge, United Kingdom
2University Bordeaux, LOMA UMR-CNRS 5798, F-33405 Talence Cedex, France
(Dated: July 18, 2019)

Spin-orbit coupling (SOC) is known to play an important role in superconductor/ferromagnet het-
erostructures. Here we demonstrate that SOC results in the spontaneous generation of vortices in an
s-wave superconductor placed below a ferromagnetic metal with intrinsic or interfacial SOC. By us-
ing the generalized London and Ginzburg-Landau theories, we calculate the supercurrent amplitude
in various types of S/F heterostructures at and below the superconducting critical temperature. In
particular, we show that even in the limit of weak SOC, vortex-antivortex pairs are stabilized and
that an attractive interaction between vortices and SOC act to pin vortices along the S/F interface.
We discuss key experiments to investigate these phenomena, which would provide a platform to the
creation of Abrikosov vortex memory.

In an s-wave superconductor (S), charge currents flows in
the absence of dissipation but since the Cooper pairs con-
sist of electrons with antiparallel spins in a spin-singlet
state, the current does not carry net spin. The oppo-
site occurs in a ferromagnetic (F) metal in which an
internal exchange field hex creates a mismatch in the
density of states of electrons with up and down spins,
meaning that charge flow with a net spin-polarization
but with with dissipitation [1]. At a S/F interface, hex
interacts within S over the superconducting coherence
length which for Nb is ОҫS = 30 вҲ’ 40 nm [2], whilst
in F the interaction is much shorter with ОҫF = 1 вҲ’ 3
nm in Co [3вҖ“5], Fe [4, 6], Ni [3вҖ“5] due to hex acting to
rapidly dephase the singlet pairs.
Consequently, there
exists a F-thickness-dependent modulation of the criti-
cal temperature Tc [7] in S/F bilayers and critical cur-
rent Ic oscillations in S/F/S Josephson junctions [8вҖ“10].
In F1/S/F2 [11вҖ“15] or F1/F2/S [16вҖ“19] spin-valves, the
total exchange field acting on the superconductivity is
controllable via magnetization-alignment of the F layers,
which creates a dependence of Tc on magnetic configu-
ration or Ic in S/F1/F2/S Josephson junctions [20вҖ“22].
Furthermore, a non-parallel alignment of magnetizations
between the F layers converts singlet pairs to a spin-
aligned triplet state [23вҖ“25].
Since spin-aligned triplet
pairs are spin-polarized, triplet supercurrents are able to
carry a net spin in addition to charge and so are stable
in F materials over length scales that exceed ОҫF [26вҖ“32].
These phenomena form the basis of applications in su-
perconducting spintronics [33].

Spin-orbit coupling (SOC) is also present at a S/F
interface due to the absence of inversion symmetry
[23, 24, 34вҖ“37] and can be enhanced by introducing an
interlayer between the S and F layers with strong SOC.
Since spin is not conserved in the presence of SOC, the
SOC locally disrupts the singlet pairs, rotating their
spins towards the direction of magnetization.
Hence,
SOC in combination with a single F layer can gener-
ate spin-aligned triplet pairs [38, 39]. For example, in
a recent ferromagnetic resonance spin-pumping experi-

ment on Pt/Nb/Py [40вҖ“42] spin-pumping efficiency was
enhanced over the normal state below Tc. These results
indicated the presence of a spin-triplet channel in Nb
that exists over ОҫS, which forms due to SOC in Pt in
combination with hex from Py.
Interfacial SOC provides a Rashba-type contribution
вҲј (вғ—Пғ Г— вғ—p) В· ЛҶn to the energy, where вғ—Пғ is the electron spin,
вғ—p is the orientation of the momentum and ЛҶn is the unit
vector along the broken inversion symmetry axis. Mag-
netic order spin-polarizes electrons, meaning that mo-
mentum along вғ—Пғ Г— ЛҶn is energetically favourable [43] and
so may induce spontaneous charge currents. This is pre-
dicted near a S/F interface within the London penetra-
tion depth from the interface [43], around magnetic is-
lands on a thin-film S [44], in a closed S loop partially
covered by a ferromagnetic insulator [45] and in a thin-
film S in contact with a NeВҙel skyrmion [46].
In this Letter we investigate the interaction of SOC
with vortices in S/F heterostructures, at temperatures
T below and at Tc.
We derive a criterion for sponta-
neous vortex generation due to SOC and show that this
is achievable using realistic materials parameters. Based
on these results, we demonstrate that SOC strongly af-
fects transport properties in S/F junctions.

In summary,
we have theoretically investigated the
role of SOC in S/F devices at and below Tc.
We
have shown that even weak SOC (Оұ вүғ 0.01/Оҫ) generates
vortex-antivortex pairs and that an attractive interaction
between vortices and SOC acts to pin vortices along S/F
interfaces. This effect would manifest experimentally as

Vortex generation and pinning are prominent
when the magnetization is perpendicular to the interface
and diminishes as the magnetization rotates in-plane
towards the S/F interface. In our calculations, we con-
sider a F metal with intrinsic SOC; however, matching
behaviour is expected when separating the SOC from the
F layer by introducing a metallic interface with intrinsic
SOC [51]. Ideal materials combinations include Pt/Co,
Pd/Co, Pt/Py and Pd/Py, which have high interfacial
transparency with Nb [52, 53]. A key breakthrough of
our theory is that it predicts the potential for vortex
guiding without the usual requirement of stray magnetic
fields to generate vortex-antivortex pairs and that vortex
motion [see Fig. 1(a)] is reversible with applied current
direction or magnetization which are key requirements
for Abrikosov memory.
