Combining non-hermiticity and interactions yields novel effects in open quantum many-body
systems. Here, we develop the generalized Schrieffer-Wolff transformation and derive the effective
Hamiltonian suitable for various quasi-degenerate non-Hermitian systems. We apply our results
to an exemplary PT ‚Äďsymmetric circuit QED composed of two non-Hermitian qubits embedded in
a lossless resonator. We consider a resonant quantum circuit as |ŌČr ‚ąí ‚Ą¶| ‚Č™ ŌČr, where ‚Ą¶ and ŌČr
are qubits and resonator frequencies, respectively, providing well-defined groups of quasi-degenerate
resonant states. For such a system, using direct numerical diagonalization we obtain the dependence
of the low-lying eigenspectrum on the interaction strength between a single qubit and the resonator,
g, and the gain (loss) parameter ő≥, and compare that with the eigenvalues obtained analytically
using the effective Hamiltonian of resonant states. We identify PT ‚Äďsymmetry broken and unbroken
phases, trace the formation of Exceptional Points of the second and the third order, and provide a
complete phase diagram g ‚ąí ő≥ of low-lying resonant states. We relate the formation of Exceptional
Points to the additional P-pseudo-Hermitian symmetry of the system and show that non-hermiticity
mixes the ‚ÄĚdark‚ÄĚ and the ‚ÄĚbright‚ÄĚ states, which has a direct experimental consequence.

I.
INTRODUCTION

Seminal
Schrieffer-Wolff
transformation
[1‚Äď3](also
known as Van Vleck Quasi-Degenerate Perturbation the-
ory [4‚Äď8] in some other contexts) has been used for many
years as an extremely useful tool in a theoretical study
of weakly interacting quantum systems. The Schrieffer-
Wolff transformation allows one to consistently derive the
effective low-energy Hamiltonian composed of the non-
interacting part and the interaction terms taken into ac-
count as a perturbation. Generally, Schrieffer-Wolf trans-
formation was successfully applied to the perturbation
analysis of various quantum-mechanical problems arising
in electronic or optical systems, such as the Kondo prob-
lem in the Anderson impurity model in normal metals or
superconductors [2, 9], multiorbital Hubbard models [10],
Floquet spectrum of periodically driven systems [11], in-
teracting qubits [12‚Äď18]. Moreover, the Schrieffer-Wolff
transformation was also adapted to intrinsically dissipa-
tive quantum systems described by Markovian master
equations [19] and, more recently, discussed in the con-
text of some non-hermitian systems [20, 21].
A particular example of the qubits-type systems coined
as circuit QED, i.e., various arrays of superconducting
qubits coupled to a low dissipative resonator, presents a
special interest for quantum computing, quantum simula-
tions, and precise quantum measurements [12, 13, 18, 22].
Even a weak coupling of qubits to the resonator leads to
numerous interesting phenomena, e.g., AC Stark shift
of qubits frequencies [12, 23], or the interaction between
well separated qubits mediated by the exchange of virtual
photons of the resonator [12, 24, 25], and the Schrieffer-
Wolff transformation are routinely used to analyze these

‚ąó Grigorii.Starkov@rub.de

effects.
Recently, this field of study has got a new twist as
so-called PT ‚Äďsymmetric non-Hermitian qubits systems
have been experimentally realized in various solid state
systems such as trapped ions, ultracold and Rydberg
atoms [26‚Äď28], Bose‚ÄďEinstein condensate [29], supercon-
ducting [30‚Äď32] or nitrogen-vacancies qubits [33].
In
a PT ‚Äďsymmetric non-Hermitian qubit, specially chosen
quantum states demonstrate a nonequilibrium growth of
the population, i.e., the states with a gain. To establish
the PT ‚Äďsymmetry the gain has to be completely equal-
ized by a loss present in other parts of a system.
A general theoretical study of PT -symmetric non-
Hermitian Hamiltonians has been started by the works
of Bender and coworkers [34‚Äď36].
They have shown
that depending on the values of physical parameters the
PT ‚Äďsymmetric non-Hermitian Hamiltonian exhibits two
kinds of energy spectrum: purely real eigenvalues, identi-
fying the unbroken (preserved) PT -symmetric quantum
phase, or complex conjugate ones, indicating the appear-
ance of broken PT ‚Äďsymmetry quantum phase. The Ex-
ceptional Points (EPs) separate these phases.
Systems of PT -symmetric interacting qubits (two-
levels or spin 1/2) have been theoretically studied for
two PT ‚Äď non-Hermitian qubits with an exchange inter-
action [37] as well as in the framework of the quantum
Ising model with a longitudinal [38, 39] or transverse non-
Hermitian fields [40]. At the same time, one should stress
that in most previous works a theoretical study of the be-
havior of eigenvalues and eigenvectors near an n-th order
EP has been carried out using the local effective n √ó n
Hamiltonian. Therefore, a study of the relation between
the EPs arising in the presence of interaction between
qubits, and the properties of non-interacting qubits sys-
tem did not receive a proper attention so far.
In this Article, we develop the generalized Schrieffer-
Wolff transformation for arbitrary non-Hermitian inter-

which the interaction is treated as the perturbation up

Since the Schrieffer-Wolff trans-

ply our generic analysis to an exemplary PT ‚Äďsymmetric
quantum system, i.e., a PT ‚Äďsymmetric circuit QED com-

The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section II

we formulate the general Schrieffer-Wolff transformation
suitable for quasi-degenerate non-Hermitian Hamiltonian
systems.
Using such transformation, we derive the ef-
fective Hamiltonian. In Section III we introduce a ba-

In Section IV, we use
the Schrieffer-Wolff transformation to derive the effec-
tive Hamiltonian matrix of the resonant states and dis-

using the effective Hamiltonian we study the formation

phase diagram of the low-lying resonant states. In Sec-
tion VI the conclusions will be provided. There are also
Appendix A, where we compute the matrix elements of
the effective Hamiltonian, and Appendices B, C and D,
where the derivation details regarding the phase diagram
and EPs are presented.

VI.
CONCLUSIONS

To conclude, we extend the formalism of the gener-
alized Schrieffer-Wolff transformation to arbitrary non-
Hermitian interacting quantum systems, and derive the
effective Hamiltonian taking into account the interaction
terms as the perturbation up to the second order. To
demonstrate the effectiveness of the method we apply this
generic procedure to a basic PT ‚Äďsymmetric circuit QED
composed of two non-Hermitian non-interacting super-
conducting qubits weakly coupled to a lossless resonator
of the frequency ŌČr. In this model the non-Hermiticity
is introduced as a staggered imaginary longitudinal mag-
netic field or gain/loss ő≥ of corresponding qubits states.
We consider PT ‚Äďsymmetric circuit QED biased in the
resonant regime as the frequency of a single qubit ‚Ą¶ is
close to the frequency of the resonator, i.e., ‚Ą¶ ‚ČÉ ŌČr.
The resonant regime of a circuit QED is a simplest one
providing well-defined groups of quasi-degenerate eigen-
states even for moderate values of the coupling strength,
g/‚Ą¶.
Using the explicit expression for the effective Hamil-
tonian, we calculate analytically the eigenspectrum of a
PT ‚Äďsymmetric circuit QED for different parameters ő≥
and g and identify the PT ‚Äďsymmetry preserved and bro-
ken quantum phases, obtain the EPs of different orders.
In particular, we obtain the values of parameters ő≥cr and
gcr for the third-order EPs. The values of ő≥cr and gcr
decrease with the detuning ŌČr ‚ąí ‚Ą¶ and the mixing angle
őł ‚Č™ 1.
We compare our analytical results with the direct nu-
merical diagonalization of the Hamiltonian (22) and find
a good agreement in the range of parameters g, ő≥ ‚Č™ 1
for both a longitudinal and transverse coupling between
qubits and the resonator.
Moreover, for PT quantum
circuits with a longitudinal coupling we find a good
agreement in a whole range of the interaction strength,
g/‚Ą¶ ‚Č§ 0.4, and as a result one can obtain the position of
the third-order EP with a great accuracy. The reason for
that is the absence of a substantial mixing between eigen-
states of different groups in PT ‚Äďquantum circuit with a
longitudinal coupling.
The most important physical signature of the non-
hermiticity is the mixture of ‚ÄĚbright‚ÄĚ and ‚ÄĚdark‚ÄĚ states.
In particular, in the absence of non-Hermitian terms the
energy spectrum of a circuit QED system splits into the
subgroups of ‚ÄĚbright‚ÄĚ and ‚ÄĚdark‚ÄĚ states having different
parity indices, and therefore, these subgroups do not mix
with each other [46]. As the non-Hermiticity is turned
on, the second-order EPs are formed between the pair of
eigenstates with different parity (i.e. the ‚ÄĚdark‚ÄĚ and a
