Berry curvature derived negative magnetoconductivity

observed in type-II magnetic Weyl semimetal films

Ayano Nakamura,1 Shinichi Nishihaya,1 Hiroaki Ishizuka,1 Markus Kriener,2

Mizuki Ohno,3 Yuto Watanabe,1 Masashi Kawasaki,2, 3 and Masaki Uchida1, ŌłŚ

2RIKEN Center for Emergent Matter Science (CEMS), Wako 351-0198, Japan

Abstract

Here we study nonmonotonic features which appear both in magnetoresistivity and anomalous

Hall resistivity during the simple magnetization process, by systematically measuring type-II mag-

netic Weyl semimetal EuCd2Sb2 films over a wide carrier density range. We find that a positive

magnetoresistivity hump can be explained as manifestation of a field-linear term in the generalized

magnetoconductivity formula including the Berry curvature. As also confirmed by model calcula-

tion, the term can be negative and pronounced near the Weyl point energy in the case that the

Weyl cones are heavily tilted. Our findings demonstrate extensive effects of the Berry curvature

on various magnetotransport in magnetic Weyl semimetals beyond the anomalous Hall effect.

Weyl semimetals (WSMs) host pairs of three-dimensional band crossing points called

Weyl points in their bulk band structure, where the spin degeneracy is resolved by break-

ing of either space-inversion or time-reversal symmetry1ŌĆō3. Magnetotransport reflecting the

Berry curvature has recently been attracting great interest in condensed matter physics4.

Owing to the large Berry curvature distribution around the Weyl points, magnetic WSMs

with time-reversal symmetry breaking exhibit giant magnetoelectric responses such as in

anomalous Hall effect (AHE)5ŌĆō7, anomalous Nernst effect (ANE)8,9, chiral anomaly10ŌĆō13, and

magneto-optical effect14. It has been also theoretically proposed that nontrivial coupling be-

tween charge and magnetization originating in the chiral anomaly enables further versatile

functionalities such as charge-induced spin torque and electric field-induced magnetization

switching15,16.

For magnetic WSMs, 3d-electron systems including Co3Sn2S217ŌĆō23, Co2MnGa24ŌĆō29, and

Mn3Sn30ŌĆō34 have been intensively studied so far. AHE and also ANE are largely enhanced

due to the presence of multiple Weyl points in the ferromagnetic ground state and ob-

served up to high temperatures near or well above room temperature6,8,9,35.
However,

their band structures with many Weyl and non-Weyl bands near the Fermi level are too

complex to accurately evaluate magnetotransport derived from the Weyl points. On the

other hand, 4f-electron magnetic WSMs including GdPtBi36ŌĆō39, EuCd2Sb240ŌĆō44, and possi-

bly EuCd2As243,45ŌĆō47 exhibit a more simple band structure, where only the bands forming

the Weyl points exist near the Fermi level in the forced ferromagnetic state. Moreover,

their semimetallic nature with low carrier densities (1018ŌĆō1019 cmŌłÆ3) as compared to those

of 3d-electron systems (1020ŌĆō1022 cmŌłÆ3) make it possible to elucidate the carrier density

dependence across the Weyl point energy. Recently, the detailed Fermi level dependence of

AHE has been examined by measuring EuCd2Sb2 thin films with incorporating electrostatic

gating48.

EuCd2Sb2 may be a suitable system for studying Berry curvature derived magnetotrans-

port in type-II magnetic WSMs49. It has a trigonal crystal structure with space group P┬»3m1,

as sketched in Fig. 1(a)43. The Cd 5s and Sb 5p bands are inverted to form a simple band

structure with type-II band crossings near the Fermi level, and the Eu 4f states provide

large isotropic magnetic moments (Eu2+: S = 7/2, L = 0)40,41. At zero magnetic field, in-

plane A-type antiferromagnetic ordering is realized below the N┬┤eel temperature TN = 7.5 K,

where a gap opened by the antiferromagnetic ordering is negligibly small and the crossing

2


points can be seen almost degenerate as Dirac points41. By applying the magnetic field B

along the c-axis, the magnetic moments are gradually canted without spin-flop transition

and are fully aligned along the c-axis above the saturation field Bs40. During the simple

magnetization process, a few pairs of Weyl points are formed by splitting of the Dirac points

and shifted in energy and momentum with the Zeeman band splitting.

While magnetotransport of 4f-electron magnetic WSMs has been studied mainly focusing

on the forced ferromagnetic state, intriguing features may appear also during the magnetiza-

tion process. One typical example is the nonmonotonic behavior observed in the anomalous

Hall resistivity below Bs48,50ŌĆō52. Furthermore, it has been theoretically proposed that the

Berry curvature can also affect magnetoconductivity or magnetoresistivity especially when

the Weyl cones are tilted as in type-II WSMs53.

Here we systematically study magnetotransport of EuCd2Sb2 thin films over a wide carrier

density range, particularly focusing on nonmonotonic features appearing below the satura-

tion field. In addition to the nonmonotonic feature in the anomalous Hall resistivity, we find

that a broad hump appears in the magnetoresistivity curve. This can be interpreted as man-

ifestation of a field-linear term in the generalized magnetoconductivity formula including the

Berry curvature.

EuCd2Sb2 single-crystalline films were grown on CdTe (111)A substrate using a molecular

beam epitaxy (MBE) technique48. In order to remove native oxide and obtain an atomi-

cally smooth surface, CdTe substrates were etched with 0.01 % Br2-methanol and heated

to 680 ŌŚ”C under Cd flux inside the MBE chamber prior to the growth54,55. EuCd2Sb2 films

were then grown at 324ŌŚ”C by codeposition of Eu, Cd, and Sb with Cd rich pressure ratios

(PCd/(PEu+PSb) Ōł╝ 30 - 60). For achieving high crystallinity, the films were annealed at 453

ŌŚ”C for 3 minutes with supplying excess Cd after the growth. Growth orientation is along

the c-axis, and the film thickness was designed at 50 nm. Since Cd is easily deficient in

EuCd2Sb2, we have obtained EuCd2Sb2 films with hole densities ranging from 1018 to 1020

cmŌłÆ3. Longitudinal resistivity Žüxx and Hall resistivity Žüyx were measured with employing

a standard four-probe method on a Hall bar. Aluminum wire was connected to the ter-

minals by using an ultrasonic bonding machine and these connections were reinforced by

appending silver paste. Low-temperature measurements of Žüxx and Žüyx up to 9 T were per-

formed using a Cryomagnetics cryostat system equipped with a superconducting magnet.

Magnetization curves up to 7 T were measured using a superconducting quantum interfer-

In summary, we have investigated the nonmonotonic behavior which appears both in

magnetoresistivity and anomalous Hall resistivity below the saturation field, by systemat-

ically measuring type-II magnetic WSM EuCd2Sb2 thin films over a wide carrier density

range. The positive magnetoresistivity hump can be interpreted as manifestation of the

negative ŽāB(1)
xx
term in the generalized magnetoconductivity formula including the Berry

curvature. This term becomes prominent when the Weyl cones are tilted, and also it can

be distinguished from the ŽāB(2)
xx
term that is always positive. Our findings highlight the

importance of the Berry curvature on various magnetotransport in magnetic WSMs beyond

the anomalous Hall effect.
