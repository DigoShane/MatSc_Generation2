Quasiparticle scattering in a superconductor near a nematic critical point: resonance mode and
multiple attractive channels.

Dimitri Pimenov,1, вҲ— Alex Kamenev,1 and Andrey V. Chubukov1

1William I. Fine Theoretical Physics Institute, University of Minnesota, Minneapolis, MN 55455, USA

We analyze the scattering rate for 2D fermions interacting via soft nematic fluctuations. The
ground state is an s-wave superconductor, but other pairing channels are almost equally attractive.
This strongly alters the scattering rate: At energies beyond the pairing gap вҲҶ, it is renormalized by
contributions from all pairing channels. At energies of order вҲҶ, it is determined by the competition
between scattering into a gapped continuum and dispersing nematic resonance. The outcome is a
вҖңpeak-peak-dip-humpвҖқ spectrum, similar, but not identical, to the вҖңpeak-dip-humpвҖқ structure in the
cuprates.

Introduction.вҖ” The spin resonance mode, observed in
a number of quasi-2D cuprate superconductors at mo-
mentum (ПҖ, ПҖ), has attracted much attention in the stud-
ies of high-temperature superconductivity [1вҖ“3]. The res-
onance mode mediates a 4-fermion interaction and ac-
counts for theвҖңpeak-dip-humpвҖқstructure of the fermionic
spectral function observed in ARPES measurements [4вҖ“
9]. In an itinerant approach, the resonance can be viewed
as a spin exciton, whose emergence below the threshold
for the continuum is associated with the d-wave sym-
metry of a gap function (see e.g., [10, 11] and references
therein).
In this Letter, we argue that a similar phenomenon
takes place in a 2D s-wave superconductor near a
quantum critical point (QCP) towards a charge nematic
order. We demonstrate that the nematic propagator pos-
sesses a charge exciton at a frequency в„Ұres(q), which near
an instability is far below the threshold for the continuum
at twice the gap value, 2вҲҶ.
A nematic resonance at
q = 0 has been observed in the B1g Raman spectra of
several iron-based superconductors [12вҖ“16]; here we show
that в„Ұres(q) and the spectral weight of the resonance are
strongly q-dependent, which is tied to the (Пү/|q|)-form
of the Landau damping of a nematic propagator in the
normal state. We analyze how the dispersing charge ex-
citon affects the fermionic spectral function at energies
above the threshold at вҲҶ+в„Ұres(q = 0). We find that the
spectral function is highly non-monotonic due to strong
momentum dependence of в„Ұres(q). In contrast, a spin
resonance in a d-wave superconductor does not lead to
such behavior as there relevant momenta q are close to
(ПҖ, ПҖ).
We further analyze the form of the spectral function
at energies above 3вҲҶ, when the key contribution comes
from the continuum in the nematic propagator. Here, we
reveal additional physics of a nematic QCP. Namely, for
an isotropic fermionic dispersion, the interaction, medi-
ated by nematic fluctuations, yields an attraction in all
pairing channels. The s-wave coupling is the largest one,
but only by a small number, proportional to the distance
to a nematic transition [17, 18]. The continuum part of

Im[ОЈR(Пү)] of fermions in an sвҲ’wave SC state near a
nematic QCP. We argued that Im[ОЈR(Пү)] is qualitatively
different from that in an ordinary s-wave SC because
near a QCP, the pairing interaction in the s-wave chan-
nel is only slightly larger than in pairing channels with
other angular momenta. We showed that the spectrum
of nematic fluctuations in a SC consists of a gapped con-
tinuum and a strongly dispersing resonance mode. We
found three different regimes of the scattering rate, due to
the interaction with a resonance: a jump at the threshold,
a decrease towards a shallow minimum, and a slow sub-
sequent increase. A fourth regime is realized at Пү вүҘ 3вҲҶ,
when the scattering rate predominantly comes from the
interaction with the continuum. At higher frequencies,
Im[ОЈR(Пү)] gradually transforms into the normal state ex-
pression. This leads to aвҖңpeak-peak-dip-humpвҖқstructure
of the fermionic spectral function. This structure trans-
forms into the more familiar peak-dip-hump lineshape as
the system comes closer to a nematic QCP and the coup-
ling constant О» increases.
Superconductivity at the onset of a nematic order at
T = 0 develops in several systems studied in recent years,
most notably in FeSe1вҲ’xSx around x = 0.2. We call for
ARPES measurements of the fermionic spectral function
to verify our results.
