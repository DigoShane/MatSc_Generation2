Magnetic properties, electrical resistivity, and hardness of high-entropy alloys FeCoNiPd
and FeCoNiPt

Jiro Kitagawa1

1 Department of Electrical Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, Fukuoka Institute of Technology,3-30-1 Wajiro-higashi, Higashi-ku, Fukuoka 811-0295, Japan

Abstract

We report the magnetic properties, electrical resistivity, and Vickers microhardness of as-cast and annealed high-entropy alloys
(HEAs) FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt with the face-centered cubic structure. The heat treatment at 800 ◦C does not largely affect the
physical properties in each HEA. The values of the Curie temperature and the saturation moment at 50 K are 955 K and 1.458
µB/f.u. for the annealed FeCoNiPd, and 851 K and 1.456 µB/f.u. for the annealed FeCoNiPt, respectively. Each HEA is a soft
ferromagnet and shows metallic resistivity. The electronic structure calculations of both HEAs support the ferromagnetic ground
states. The comparisons between experimental and theoretical values are made for the Curie temperature, the saturation moment,
and the residual resistivity. The Vickers microhardness of annealed FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt are both 188 HV. The hardness vs.
valence electron count (VEC) per atom plot of these HEAs does not largely deviate from an expected universal relation forming a
broad peak at VEC∼6.8. This study would give some hints on designing a soft ferromagnetic HEA with high hardness.

Keywords: High-entropy alloy, Soft ferromagnetism, Electrical resistivity, Hardness

1. Introduction

High-entropy alloys (HEAs) receive much interest due to
their rich functionality, such as high strength, corrosion resis-
tance, energy storage, radiation protection, superconductivity,
soft ferromagnetism, and biocompatibility[1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8].
The entropy state of an alloy is classified by the configurational
entropy ∆S mix = −R �n
i=1 cilnci, where n is the number of ele-
ments, ci is the atomic fraction, and R is the gas constant. At the
initial stage of HEAs research, HEA required ∆S mix larger than
1.62 R, which can be realized by the solid solution of more than
five elements. The critical value defining HEA is now[9] 1.0
R. So the equiatomic quaternary alloy investigated in this study
can be referred to as HEA. In HEAs, the microstructure often
affects the physical properties, and, for example, the control of
microstructure towards improved mechanical or magnetic prop-
erties is the central topic of HEAs[10, 11]. The other current
topic is the materials research on HEAs exhibiting novel phe-
nomena [12, 13] by utilizing the large compositional space of
HEAs.
Magnetic HEAs are attracting much attention because of
their good soft ferromagnetism coexisted with high strength
and/or high hardness[7, 14]. The magnetic HEAs are a promis-
ing alternative to conventional soft magnetic materials with
poor strength[7].
Based on a face-centered cubic (fcc) Fe-
CoNi with high saturation magnetization and low coercive field
Hc, many studies on the effect of adding alloying elements are
carried out. For example, AlxCoCrFeNi is well investigated

enhanced above room temperature as x increases[15]. NiFe-
CoCrPd and NiFeCoCrMn are famous equiatomic HEAs with
TC=440 K and 38 K, respectively[16, 17]. There are a few
reports which aim at attaining a higher Hc. FeCoNiAlCuxTix
shows a relatively high Hc of 955 Oe after heat treatment[18].
The recent research on magnetic HEAs shows a growing in-
terest in controlling magnetic properties through tailoring a
microstructure[19, 11]. For example, Fe15Co15Ni20Mn20Cu30
exhibits a spinodal decomposition after a heat treatment[11],
which leads to enhanced TC.
We are focusing on HEAs with the combination of 3d mag-
netic elements and noble metals (e.g., Rh, Ir, Pd, and Pt)[20]
because materials research on such an HEA is unexplored. Re-
cently, Fukushima et al. reported the database (TC, spin mo-
ment, residual resistivity) of 147630 quaternary HEAs pro-
duced by a density functional theory calculation[21].
This
database contains fcc FeCoNiPd and fcc FeCoNiPt, which can
be regarded as Pd- or Pt-added FeCoNi alloy, and the magnetic
properties of these HEAs are not well investigated. Only the
M¨ossbauer effect of FeCoNiPd is reported[22], and the funda-
mental magnetic properties such as TC and saturation moment
Ms are unknown. Therefore, this study’s first purpose is to as-
sess the fundamental magnetic properties of fcc FeCoNiPd and
fcc FeCoNiPt and to compare the magnetic properties between
these HEAs and FeCoNi.

the heat treatment does not significantly influence the physical
properties of FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt. The soft ferromagnetic
behaviors are observed in both HEAs. The comparisons be-
tween experimental and theoretical values are made for TC, Ms,
and the residual resistivity. The hardness of FeCoNiPd and Fe-
CoNiPt do not deviate from the empirical relationship between
the hardness and the VEC.

2. Materials and Methods

Polycrystalline samples of FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt were
synthesized by a home-made arc furnace using constituent el-
ements Fe (99.9 %), Co (99.9 %), Ni (99.9 %), Pd (99.9 %),
and Pt (99.9 %) under Ar atmosphere. The button-shaped sam-
ples were remelted several times on a water-cooled Cu hearth
and flipped each time to ensure homogeneity. The as-cast sam-
ples were annealed in an evacuated quartz tube at 800 ◦C for
four days. Room temperature X-ray diffraction (XRD) patterns
of samples were recorded using an X-ray diffractometer (XRD-
7000L, Shimadzu) with Cu-Kα radiation. We used thin slabs
cut from the samples due to their high ductility. The scanning
electron microscope (SEM) images were collected using a field
emission scanning electron microscope (FE-SEM, JSM-7100F,
JEOL). The chemical composition was also evaluated by an en-
ergy dispersive X-ray (EDX) spectrometer equipped with the
FE-SEM.
The temperature dependence of dc magnetization χdc (T) be-
tween 50 and 300 K was measured using VersaLab (Quantum
Design). The high-temperature χdc (T) from 300 K to 1173
K was measured by a vibrating sample magnetometer (TM-
VSM33483-HGC, Tamakawa) to estimate TC. The isothermal
magnetization (M) curve at 50 K was taken using the VersaLab.
The temperature dependence of electrical resistivity ρ (T) be-
tween 3 K and 300 K was measured by a conventional dc four-
probe method using a home-made sysytem in a GM refrigerator

We also performed the electronic structure calculation us-

from Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (PBE) and treated the spin-

teraction is not included in the previous study[21].

However, FeCoNiPd or FeCoNiPt forms a stable single-phase
fcc against the heat treatment at 800 ◦C.
Figures 3(a) and (b) show χdc (T) under the external field
H of 100 Oe for FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt, respectively. In
each sample, a steep increase of χdc is observed as the tem-
perature is lowered, which indicates a ferromagnetic ordering.
TC is estimated by the temperature derivative of χdc and de-
fined by the minimum point described in the inset of Fig.3(a)
or (b). This is one of the effective ways to obtain TC in transi-
tion metal-based ferromagnets[27, 28, 29, 30]. Thus obtained
TCs are listed in Table 1, and the slight enhancement of TC after
the annealing is confirmed in each HEA. Fukushima et al. have
provided TC data obtained by the density functional theory cal-
culation for 147630 quaternary HEAs and placed the data in a
repository[31]. The predicted TCs of fcc FeCoNiPd and fcc Fe-
CoNiPt are 1137 and 1085 K, respectively. Although the higher
TC of FeCoNiPd compared to FeCoNiPt is consistent with the
experimental result, the theoretical value is approximately 200
K higher than the experimental one in each HEA. The mean-
field approximation used for calculating TC tends to overesti-
mation, which is the reason for the relatively large difference
between theoretical and experimental values. The isothermal
M-H curves of FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt measured at 50 K are
exhibited in Figs.3(c) and (d). With the increase of H from
0 Oe in each HEA, M increases steeply and soon saturates,
which supports the ferromagnetic ground state. Thermal an-
nealing seems not to affect M-H curves. The values of Ms for
HEAs investigated are summarized in Table 1 and compared
with the theoretical values below. The inset of each figure is
the expanded view to show the hysteresis. The very weak hys-
teresis indicates the soft ferromagnetism in each HEA. While
Hc of FeCoNiPt would be approximately 2 Oe, no hysteresis is
observed for FeCoNiPd within the measurement accuracy.
Here we compare the magnetic properties of FeCoNiPd and
FeCoNiPt with those of fcc FeCoNi. The lattice parameter,
TC, and Ms of fcc FeCoNi are reported to be 3.599 Å, 1000
K, and 163 emu/g (=1.687 µB/f.u.), respectively[32, 33]. The
atomic radius of Pd(Pt) is 1.3754 Å(1.387 Å), which is larger
than those of Fe, Co, and Ni (1.2412, 1.2510, and 1.2459

Å, respectively)[34]. Therefore, the lattice parameter of Fe-
CoNiPd or FeCoNiPt expands compared to FeCoNi. As dis-
cussed later, Pd(Pt) carries the magnetic moment smaller than
Fe, Co, and Ni moments, which causes the reduction of Ms in
FeCoNiPd or FeCoNiPt. Going from FeCoNi, FeCoNiPd to
FeCoNiPt, the lattice parameter increases, and TC is systemat-
ically reduced. The expansion of lattice means the increase of
interatomic distance between 3d elements, which would lead
to a weakened magnetic exchange interaction. In this case, the
systematic reduction of TC with increasing lattice parameter can
be anticipated. We note that FeCoNiCr (a=3.580 Å, TC=104
K)[35, 36] and FeCoNiMn (a=3.6029 Å, TC=332 K)[37] sig-
nificantly reduce TC, which is independent of the unit-cell vol-
ume change compared to FeCoNi.
The alloying by antifer-
romagnetic elements of Cr or Mn severely decreases the av-
eraged magnetic exchange interaction strength. FeCoNi pos-
sesses Hc of 1.5 Oe and this value is not largely enhanced in
fcc FeCoNiAlx or fcc FeCoNiSix (Hc: 0.5∼6 Oe)[7]. The same
trend is observed in FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt, and the soft fer-
romagnetism of fcc FeCoNi is robust against the addition of
alloying elements.
Next, Ms and Hc of FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt are compared
with those of related systems. The values of Ms in the unit
of emu/g for FeCoNiPd and FeCoNiPt after annealing are 116
emu/g and 88 emu/g, respectively.
The corresponding val-
ues of FeCoNi-related systems[38, 39, 32, 40] are, for exam-
ple, 130 emu/g in FeCoNiAlx (x=0.2 and 0.3), 126 emu/g in
FeCoNiSi0.25, and 101 emu/g in FeCoNiAl0.25Mn0.25. So, while
Ms of FeCoNiPd is slightly reduced compared to FeCoNiAlx
and FeCoNiSi0.25, the relatively large reduction of Ms occurs in
FeCoNiPt due to the heavy element Pt with a small magnetic
moment. This comparison indicates that light elements are fa-
vorable for achieving high Ms soft ferromagnet. FeCoNiAlx
(x=0.2 and 0.3), FeCoNiSi0.25, and FeCoNiAl0.25Mn0.25 pos-
sess Hc ranging from 0.5 to 5 Oe[38, 39, 32, 40]. These val-
ues are comparable to Hc (∼2 Oe) of FeCoNiPt. On the other
hand, Hc of FeCoNiPd would be smaller compared to the re-
lated systems. Hc of HEAs and commercial soft ferromagnets
are summarized in the review by Huang et al[41]. According to
the review, Hc of FeCoNiPd would locate in the range of com-
mercial soft ferromagnets (Ni-Fe alloys with Hc of 0.004 Oe ∼
0.1 Oe).
Figure 4 summarizes ρ (T) of as-cast and annealed Fe-
CoNiPd and FeCoNiPt. For each HEA, ρ of the as-cast sample
decreases after the heat treatment, which suggests a relaxation
of the lattice distortion. Despite the existence of atomic disor-
ders, ρ smoothly decreases with cooling. The measured temper-
ature range is well below TC, and relatively large temperature
dependences mean the dominance of magnetic contribution to
the electrical transport. Such a behavior is often observed in fer-
romagnetic metals with atomic disorders[42]. The database[31]
by Fukushima et al. also includes the values of residual resis-
tivity ρres. According to the database, ρress of fcc FeCoNiPd
and fcc FeCoNiPt are 5.1 and 21.6 µΩcm, respectively, which
agree well with the experimental values of annealed HEAs.

Figure 6 (a) depicts the Vickers microhardness vs. applied
load profiles for all samples. The hardness in each sample grad-
ually decreases as the load is increased, which is also reported
in many HEAs and intermetallic compounds[43, 44]. It is well
known that the elastic recovery effect is responsible for the in-
crease of Vickers microhardness with decreasing load[45]. In
FeCoNiPd, the annealed sample displays lower hardness than
the as-cast sample. The annealing would cause the release of
lattice distortion introduced in the rapid solidification process
of arc melting, which leads to lower hardness. FeCoNiPt also
shows similar behavior. It is proposed that the Vickers micro-
hardness depends on the VEC. Figure 6(b) presents the VEC
dependence of hardness. The solid curve is a guideline ob-
tained for HEAs with the bcc structures (VEC: 6.0∼7.55) and
fcc structures (VEC: 7.8∼8.8)[23]. The result of refractory bcc
HEAs with VEC:4.14∼5.65 is also shown[46, 47, 48, 49, 50]
and seems to be connected to the guideline.
In addition, a
deep learning study of the hardness of refractory HEAs with
the VEC: 4∼6 also supports the positive correlation between
the hardness and VEC in that VEC range[51]. The VEC of
FeCoNiPd or FeCoNiPt is 9.25, and the hardness of 188 HV
obtained at 4.9030 N load is employed because the hardness
at a higher load is usually employed. In addition we cannot
obtain the hardness under a load higher than 4.9030 N due to
the limitation of the microhardness tester used. Thus plotted
data points in Figure 6(b) do not essentially deviate from the
expected universal relation between the VEC and the hardness.
We note that there are not enough data points from other HEAs
at VEC larger than 9. Therefore, further study is required to
prove the universal relation. This study implies a vital role of
VEC in designing the hardness of magnetic HEAs. The VEC
is also related to the phase stability of fcc and bcc HEAs[52]:

4. Summary

We have investigated the fundamental magnetic properties,
electrical resistivity, and Vickers microhardness of as-cast and
annealed fcc FeCoNiPd and fcc FeCoNiPt. After the heat treat-
ment at 800 ◦C, both HEAs keep the single-phase fcc, and the
annealing does not largely alter the physical properties. The
values of TC and Ms at 50 K are 955 K and 1.458 µB/f.u. for an-
nealed FeCoNiPd, and 851 K and 1.456 µB/f.u. for annealed Fe-
CoNiPt, respectively. The coercive fields of both HEAs are very
small, and they are soft ferromagnets. In each HEA, the electri-
cal resistivity shows the metallic temperature dependence. The

6


electronic structure calculations of both HEAs were performed,
and the ferromagnetic ground states were obtained. The total
moments are close to those reported in the database made by
the density functional theory calculation and agree with the ex-
perimental Mss. The theoretical values of TC and ρres are also
reported in the database. While the theoretical TC values are rel-
atively higher than the experimental ones in both HEAs, good
agreement is confirmed between the theoretical and experimen-
tal ρres values. The database would be beneficial for the mate-
rials research on magnetic quarternary HEAs. In addition, the
comparisons of magnetic properties of HEAs investigated and
FeCoNi are made. The Vickers microhardness of FeCoNiPd
or FeCoNiPt slightly decreases after the annealing. The hard-
ness vs. VEC plot of these HEAs does not essentially deviate
from the expected universal relation forming a broad peak at
VEC∼6.8. This study provides a possible material design of
soft ferromagnetic HEA with high hardness; a magnetic HEA
based on FeCoNi with an alloying for tuning VEC=8 is promis-
ing.
