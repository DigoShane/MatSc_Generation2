Unconventional pressure dependence of the superfluid density in the nodeless
topological superconductor α–PdBi2

Debarchan Das,1, ∗ Ritu Gupta,1 Christopher Baines,1 Hubertus Luetkens,1

Dariusz Kaczorowski,2 Zurab Guguchia,1 and Rustem Khasanov1, †

1Laboratory for Muon Spin Spectroscopy, Paul Scherrer Institute, CH-5232 Villigen PSI, Switzerland
2Institute of Low Temperature and Structure Research,

Ok´olna 2, 50-422, Poland

We investigated the superconducting properties of the topological superconductor α–PdBi2 at
ambient and external pressures up to 1.77 GPa using muon spin rotation (µSR) experiments. The
ambient pressure measurements evince a fully gapped s-wave superconducting state in the bulk of
the specimen. AC magnetic susceptibility and µSR measurements manifest a continuous suppression
of Tc with increasing pressure. In parallel, we observed a significant decrease of superfluid density
by ∼20% upon application of external pressure. Remarkably, the superfluid density follows linear
relation with Tc which was found before in some unconventional topological superconductors and
hole doped cuprates. This finding signals a possible crossover from BEC to BCS in α–PdBi2.

Topological superconductors (TSCs) possess a full
pairing gap in the bulk and gapless surface states pro-
tected by time-reversal symmetry (TRS) [1–4]. The gen-
eral interest to search for novel TSC is associated with its
intimate connection to Majorana bound states as low en-
ergy excitation [4, 5] which have potential applications in
quantum computation [6, 7]. Thus, TSC has attracted
a significant research interest in recent time due to its
scientific importance and potential applications prospect.
Despite serious research efforts, the experimental realiza-
tions of bulk topological superconductivity in real mate-
rials remain considerably limited to few classes of ma-
terials, such as Cu/Sr/Nb-doped Bi2Se3 [8–13], layered
transition metal dichalcogenides [14–17], ternary transi-
tion metal pnictides [18, 64], In-doped SnTe [20] and few
half-Heusler compounds [21, 22]. Interestingly, recent re-
ports investigating various members of Pd-Bi family rein-
vigorate the interest to search for potential TSCs in this
class of materials.
Due to their intrinsic capability to
maintain strong spin orbit coupling (SOC), Pd-Bi family
turns out to be quite promising. For instance, noncen-
trosymmetric α-PdBi with a superconducting transition
at Tc = 3.8 K [23] exhibits Dirac cone electronic disper-
sion at 700 meV [24, 25] below the Fermi energy. Simi-
larly, the compound β-PdBi2 is another member of the
Pd-Bi family which has been reported to possibly host
topological superconducting state below Tc = 5.3 K [26–
28]. The Dirac point in this material is located at 2.41 eV
below the Fermi energy [29–32]. In this framework, the
relatively new member of Pd-Bi family, α-PdBi2, crys-
tallizing in monoclinic centrosymmetric structure (space
group C2/m), turns out to be very promising due to its
superconducting (Tc = 1.7 K) as well as nontrivial topo-
logical properties [33–35].
Recent ARPES results [35] on this compound reveals
the presence of a Dirac point at 1.26 eV below the Fermi

energy at the zone center.
Notably, the ARPES data
also display multiple band crossings at the Fermi energy.
This tempts us to speculate that the superconducting gap
structure might consist of multiple gaps. However, the
tunnel diode oscillator (TDO) based penetration depth
study [33] indicates an isotropic gap structure in this
compound with the gap to Tc ratio, ∆(0)/kBTc ∼ 2.0.
However, it must be noted that TDO studies are very
sensitive to surface superconductivity.
Therefore, it is
desired to probe the superconducting gap structure by
means of a bulk experimental technique. Further, Zhou
et al.[36] recently reported a very interesting pressure
(p)- Tc phase diagram in this compound manifesting a
sudden increase in Tc at p= 1.3 GPa. Interestingly, X-
ray studies under pressure reveals no structural change
around this pressure. Thus, it is very important to un-
derstand the origin of the rise in Tc and to check whether
the superfluid density also shows such an enhancement
with pressure. In this regard, muon spin rotation (µSR)
experiments at ambient and as well as under different
hydrostatic pressures are of great importance to address
these aforementioned issues.

In this letter, we report the results of our detailed µSR
experiments on α-PdBi2 at ambient and under different
hydrostatic pressures. Ambient pressure measurements
substantiate single gap s-wave superconductivity with
preserved TRS state in this compound.
Furthermore,
by performing AC-magnetic susceptibility and µSR ex-
periments under different hydrostatic pressures, we ex-
plored the pressure- Tc phase diagram which is notice-
ably different from the previous report by Zhou et al.[36].
Our results manifest a subtantial and continious decrease
of superfluid density (∼20%) and Tc with application of
pressure up to 1.77 GPa.

of α-PdBi2 obtained using melt growth technique [33]
were used for the present study. Ambient pressure and
high pressure TF µSR experiments were performed on

Figure 1a depicts TF-µSR asymmetry spectra for α-
PdBi2 at two selected temperatures above (2.5 K) and
below (0.08 K) Tc, measured in an applied magnetic field
of 10 mT. Above Tc, the spectra exhibits a very small
relaxation due to the presence of random local fields as-
sociated with the nuclear magnetic moments.
On the
contrary, in the superconducting state, the formation of
flux line lattice (FLL) produces an inhomogeneous dis-
tribution of magnetic field (inset of Fig. 1b) which in-
creases the relaxation rate of the µSR signal. Figure 1b
shows the normalised Fourier spectra for single-crystal α-
PdBi2 at temperatures above (2.5 K) and below (0.08 K)
Tc obtained by means of the maximum entropy Fourier
transform technique. While we observed a sharp peak in
the Fourier amplitude around 10 mT (external applied
field) at 2.5 K confirming homogeneous field distribution

throughout the sample, a fairly asymmetric broad signal
was seen at 0.08 K evincing the fact that the sample is in-
deed in the superconducting mixed state. The formation
of the FLL causes this broadening of the line shape.
The temperature dependence of σsc(T) for α-PdBi2
measured at an applied field of µ0H = 10 mT is pre-
sented in Fig. 1c. From the observed temperature de-
pendence of σsc(T), the nature of the superconducting
gap can be determined as σsc(T) is proportional to the
superfluid density. The solid red and blue lines in Fig. 1c
represent fitting of the experimental data with isotropic
s-wave and d-wave models [42–44], respectively(see SM
[49]). It is quite evident from the figure that the observed
σsc(T) excludes presence of any nodes and it can be best
described using single isotropic gap (s-wave) yielding a
gap value of ∆(0) = 0.18(1) meV and Tc = 1.27(3). In
this context, it is to be noted that the Tc obtained from
µSR measurements is slightly lower than that determined
from heat capacity measurements. This is because of the
fact that µSR measurements were carried out in an ap-
plied field of 10 mT which slightly lowered the super-
conducting transition temperature. From µSR measure-
ments we obtained the superconducting gap to Tc ratio as
∆(0)/kBTc = 1.64(1), which is very close to the value ex-
pected (1.764) for BCS superconductors. It is worthwhile
to emphasize that previous ARPES and band structure
calculations [34, 35] evince the presence of a Rashba state
near the Fermi energy in this compound. It is theoreti-
cally predicted that in presence of an in-plane magnetic
field, the Majorana zero mode can be realized utilizing
the coupling of an s-wave superconductor with a material
exhibiting Rashba states [50]. Thus, the observation of
s-wave superconductivity in α-PdBi2 is quite crucial in
search for Majorana fermions.

176(2) nm, respectively. Thus obtained value of µ0Hc2(0)
and λeff are in reasonably good agreement with the pre-
vious reports [33, 36]. λeff is related to the superconduct-

Notably, in case of α-PdBi2, we observe a clear de-

BCS superconductors. For comparison, we included few

Moreover, we observed a linear dependence between Tc
and the superfluid density for α-PdBi2 (purple dashed
line in Fig. 4(a)). Similar linear dependence was also ob-
served in case of another TSC MoTe2. In this context,
it should be emphasised that the nearly linear relation-
ship was originally observed in hole-doped cuprates in the
underdoped region of the phase diagram [57, 58]. Such
linear relationship is expected only in the Bose-Einstein
condensation (BEC)-like picture [57, 59, 60]. However,
in case of cuprates the ratio between Tc and their effec-
tive Fermi temperature TF is about Tc/TF ∼ 0.05, which
suggests about four to five times reduction of Tc from
the ideal BEC temperature for noninteracting Bose gas.
These results were discussed in terms of the crossover
from BEC to BCS-like condensation. Following the same
line of reasoning, we anticipate similar mechanism in α-
PdBi2.

factors determining Tc is the superfluid density. Consid-
ering the linear dependency between Tc and superfluid
density, we speculate that the suppression of Tc is a result
of reduction of superfluid density in this compound. An-

In summary, we have performed TF and ZF-µSR mea-
surements on a topological superconductor candidate α-
PdBi2. TF-µSR results reveal absence of any nodes in
the superconducting gap structure of this compound and
it can be best described by a fully gaped s-wave model.
Observation of s-wave superconductivity in this system
along with its unique band structure and Fermi surface
topology, as demonstrated previously, endorse this com-
pound as a promising candidate in search for Majorana
zero modes. Further, our ZF-µSR study manifests that
time reversal symmetry is preserved in the superconduct-
ing state of α-PdBi2. Interestingly, ACS and TF-µSR
experiments under different hydrostatic pressures, reveal
a negative pressure effect on Tc which is suppressed at an
rate of -0.10 k/GPa, upon application of pressure up to
1.77 GPa. Notably, the superconducting gap value and
the superfluid density also decrease with increasing pres-
sure up to 1.77 GPa. Most intriguing observation of the
present study is the linear dependence between Tc and

superfluid density manifesting unconventional supercon-
ductivity in this compound. This also signals possible
crossover from BEC to BCS in this compound.
Fur-
ther experimental and theoretical works under hydro-
static pressure condition are called for to fully compre-
hend the pressure dependent phase diagram of α-PdBi2.
Our results on α-PdBi2 indicating a substantial content
of novel physics in this compound opens more prospects
for studies on TSC and its potential application.

A.
Sample preparation for µSR measurements

In order to characterize the single crystals of α-PdBi2 ,
we performed preliminary investigation using single crys-
tal X-ray diffraction, electrical resistivity (ρ) and heat
capacity (C) measurements. Fig 1(a) shows the temper-
ature dependence of the heat capacity, C(T), (presented
as C/T vs T 2) at zero and applied field of 80 mT. In the
inset, we presented ρ(T) highlighting the superconduct-
ing transition in this material. A prominent anomaly in
C/T vs T 2 curve seen at Tc corroborates the bulk su-
perconductivity in this compound. C(T) data at 80 mT,
can be well modeled by C(T)/T = γn + βT 2 (solid red
line in Fig 1(a)) yielding the normal state Sommerfeld
coefficient γn = 3.6(1) mJ/mol K2 and the coefficient
β = 0.84(1) mJ/mol K4 which in turn provides an es-
timate of Debye temperature [1] ΘD = 190(1) K. Using
McMillan theory [2], we estimated the value of electron-
phonon coupling parameter λe−ph = 0.54(2).
A simi-
lar value was also seen in some other superconductors
LaPt2Si2 (λe−ph = 0.53), ThCoSi (λe−ph = 0.52), 1T-
PdTe2 (λe−ph = 0.58) [3–5]. This value of λe−ph is in-
dicative of weak electron-phonon coupling in α-PdBi2.
In this context, it is interesting to note that λe−ph for
α-PdBi2 is much smaller compared to that for β-PdBi2
(λe−ph = 3.66) determined using first principle calcula-
tion [6].
One small plate like piece (5 mm×6 mm×1 mm) con-
sisting of many single crystals (not cleaved) was mounted
on a silver holder for measurements at HAL-9500 (see
Fig 1(b)). Magnetic field was applied perpendicular to
the plane of this plate like piece.
On the other hand,
many such pieces were used for high pressure measure-
ments on GPD using a double-wall piston cylinder type
of cell with inner channel made of CuBe material. The
filling factor of the pressure cell was maximized by ac-
commodating as much sample as possible. The fraction
of the muons stopping in the sample was approximately
45%. We would like to emphasize that at room temper-
ature, Daphne oil which was used as pressure transmit-
ting medium, solidifies at a pressure of ∼2.3 GPa [7]. As

C.
Reversibility check of pressure effects in
α-PdBi2

In the previous high pressure study, Zhou et al.[14]
observed clear irreversible pressure induced effects in α-
PdBi2 while releasing pressure from 42.9 GPa. Pressure
dependent structural investigation suggested that there
is a transformation from alpha to beta phase at 8 GPa.
Even though in our present study the highest applied
was 1.77 GPa which is much smaller than in ref. [14],
it was essential to check whether the pressure induced
changes of superconducting properties observed by us,
were reversible or irreversible. In order to address this
question, we measured temperature dependent TF-µSR
measurements after releasing the pressure to 0 GPa from
1.77 GPa keeping the sample in the pressure cell. As can
be seen from Fig. 2, σSC(T) data obtained before closing
and after releasing the pressure overlaps with each other
evidencing that the pressure induced (up to 1.77 GPa)
changes of superconducting properties in α-PdBi2 re-
ported in this paper, are reversible.
