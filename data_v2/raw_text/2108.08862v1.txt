Recently the interest in superconductor/ferromagnetic
insulator (S/FI) structures has been growing rapidly.
Experimental advances in fabrication of S/FI systems
has resulted in the observation of the spin-split spec-
trum of Bogolubov quasiparticles in Al/EuS systems1–4.
The combination of the effective Zeeman field gen-
erated by the magnetic proximity effect and super-
conductivity leads to the long-range spin transport in
EuS/Al systems5–8, non-trivial spin pumping effects
in NbN/GdN9 and Nb/yttrium iron garnet (YIG)10

structures.
Moreover recently the InAs/Al/EuS mul-
tilayers combining superconductivity,
ferromagnetism
and spin-orbit coupling (SOC) have been proposed as
the platforms for the realization of the topological
superconductivity11,12.
In this Letter we demonstrate that the interplay of
superconductivity, ferromagnetism and SOC generates a
non-trivial ground state characterized by the chiral spin
order accompanied by the spatial modulation of the su-
perconducting order parameter. Such state which we call
the chiral pair density wave (CPDW) state can occur
in S/FI systems with interfacial SOC induced by heavy
metal Pt layers as shown in Fig.1 as well as in a wider
class of magnetic superconductors with spin-singlet order
parameter13–16 .
The CPDW states break the parity symmetry and
have several other qualitatively different features com-
pared to the previously studied cryptoferromagnetic
states17 in the weakly magnetic superconductors18 and
ultra-thin metallic ferromagnetic films on top of the
superconductor19,20.
The
mechanism
which
provides
the
forma-
tion
of
CPDW
state
is
the
Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya
interaction21–23 (DMI) generated by the superconduct-
ing spin currents.
The corresponding DMI interaction
energy has the form

FDM = |ψ|2dk · (m × ∇km)
(1)

where m = m(r) is the magnetic texture, d1,2,3 are the
DMI vectors and ψ is the s-wave spin-singlet supercon-
ducting order parameter. The interaction between the
superconductivity and magnetism provided by Eq.(1) is
determined by the magnetization gradients.
Therefore
it is conceptually distinct from the mechanism which in-
volves superconducting phase gradients discussed in the
context of the Edelstien effect24–27, helical superconduct-
ing states28,29, spontaneous currents30,31 and the anoma-
lous Josephson effect32.
The DMI provides the formation of magnetic spirals
and skyrmions33,34 which are considered as the building
blocs of the future spintronics devices35. Therefore con-
trolling DMI with external parameters such as the volt-
age and strain attracts significant attention36–39.
The
superconductivity-induced DMI (1) provides a plethora
of different effects resulting in the control over the mag-
netic textures with the help of the superconducting cor-
relations.

to the known non-homogeneous ground states. (i) The
CPDW state breaks the parity symmetry having definite
sign of magnetic chirality. (ii) The typical cryptoferro-
magnetic state has17–19 the period ≪ ξ0 while for the
CPDW it is Lpdw ≥ ξ0 where ξ0 is the Cooper pair size.
(iii) The transition to the CPDW state occurs via the
penetration of defects - chiral domain walls in contrast to
the second order phase transition to the cryptoferromag-
netic phase20. (iv) In contrast to the cryptoferromag-
netic state which has been observed only in the exotic
compounds18 such hybrid chiral states can be obtained
in the existing FI/S structures such as YIG/Nb/Pt or
EuS/Al/Pt multilayers. The CPDW state is also quali-
tatively different from the previously known Fulde-Ferrel-
Larkin-Ovchinnikov state60,61and the phase-modulated
superconducting state28,29,32 because in the CPDW state
the proliferation of the inhomogeneity occurs coherently
in the superconducting and magnetic subsystems.
